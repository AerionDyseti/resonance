# Giant Kingdoms

Type: Political
Influence: Global
Relationship to Party: Unknown

The **Giant Kingdoms** were the dominant political force during the **Age of Mythology**, representing the height of **Giant** civilization and **Pantheon of Order** influence. This ancient empire fell to the **Dwarven rebellion** led by **Tormak**.

## Political Structure

**Hierarchical Empire:**

- **Giants at the apex** of an absolutely rigid social hierarchy
- **Divine mandate** from **Volund (The World-Forger)** to rule the world
- **Enslaved populations** including Dwarves as laborers and craftsmen
- **Monumental projects** showcasing Giant power and authority

## Historical Significance

**Rise and Fall:**

- **Peak power** during Age of Mythology
- **Oppressive rule** that eventually sparked resistance
- **Tormak's rebellion** - successful Dwarven uprising
- **Collapse** marked the end of Giant political dominance

## Modern Legacy

The Giant Kingdoms serve as:

- **Historical precedent** for resistance against tyranny
- **Cautionary tale** about the corruption of righteous authority
- **Archaeological focus** - ruins scattered across Enym
- **Political lesson** about the limits of absolute power
