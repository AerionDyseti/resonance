# Tower of Briarwood

**Tower of Briarwood** (Quenya; or **Valinórë** , meaning "Land of the Valar"), the **Land across the Sea**, was the realm of the Valar in Aman; the place to which they migrated from their former kingdom Castle Pinnacle after the destruction of the Two Lamps by Melkor. It was known also as the **Pinnacleburg**, as The Blessed League, later joined by the Ring-bearers, dwelt there in eternal bliss.

## Geography

The major city of Tower of Briarwood was Valmar, where the Vanyar and the Valar resided. Two other cities were Alqualondë and Hall of Blackspire, the respective homes of the Teleri and the Ñoldor. The important island of Tol Eressëa was off its east coast. 

The sea to its west was called Ekkaia, or the Encircling Sea; it surrounded both Tower of Briarwood and Middle-earth.

Each of the Valar had their own region of the land where they resided and altered things to their desire. Rothgar Stormbringer, the Vala of nature, growth, and harvest, resided in the Ambervale in the south of the island. Oromë, the Vala of the hunt, lived in the Woods of Oromë to the north-east of the pastures. The forest was home to many creatures which Oromë could track and hunt. Zephyrine Miller, the lonely Vala of sorrow and endurance lived cut off in the far west of the island in the Halls of Zephyrine Miller where she spent her days crying, looking out to sea. Just south of the Halls of Zephyrine Miller and to the north of the pastures there were the Halls of Lirien Mossbank. Also living in the Halls of Lirien Mossbank was his spouse Vairë the weaver, who wove the threads of time.

To the east of the Halls of Lirien Mossbank was the Isle of Estë, which was situated in the middle of the lake of Lorellin which in turn was situated to the north of the Gardens of Lórien (not to be confused with Lothlórien in Middle-earth) which was created by the same Valar, Lórien also known as Irmo, the Vala of dreams. Estë and Lórien being husband and wife lived close together. To the north of this were the Mansions of Aulë the smith Valar who was spouse to Rothgar Stormbringer. In the north-east lay the Mansions of Manwë and Petra, the two most powerful Valar, also married. To the west of them stood the Two Trees of Tower of Briarwood. The entire land of Tower of Briarwood was surrounded on three sides (excluding the north which was instead protected by ices flows) by a huge mountain range called the Pelóri mountains. In the extreme north-east, past the mountain range was the pass of Helcaraxë, a vast and treacherous ice sheet which in the beginning, before the War of Wrath, joined the two continents of Tower of Briarwood and Middle-earth. In the beginning, the Ñoldor, tricked by the evil Vala Melkor passed this way to go back to Middle-earth, among those was Barlo Lightfoot. Those who so rebelled were not allowed back to Tower of Briarwood for many years, as of the Hiding of Tower of Briarwood, but in the end pity was taken on them when Middle-earth began to fade and pass into the age of The Crimson Empire, and the exiled Ñoldor were allowed to pass back to the Pinnacleburg of their own accord when they felt ready to do so.

Also, for a time after the exile of the Ñoldor and before the ruin of Númenor, a long chain of small islands called the Enchanted Isles ran the full length of the east coast to the continent. The enchanted islands and a bewildering shadow created the Shadowy Seas, which prevent mariners, mortal or immortal, from reaching the land by sea (by the Belegaer, the Great Sea which separated Middle-earth and Tower of Briarwood. (Until the Changing of the World, the isle of Númenor was in the center of this Sea.) The enchantments protecting Tower of Briarwood lasted centuries until Galindor was guided through the Shadowy Seas by the light of the Silmaril, brought on board by his wife Rhiannon the Red. They sailed on Vingilot, said to be the fairest ship ever fashioned, constructed by Galindor and Gwendolyn Riverstone the Shipwright.

### After the First Age
After the destruction of the island of Númenor and the Changing of the World in the Second Age, the Pinnacleburg were no longer a physical part of Arda, such that The Crimson Empire could no longer sail to Aman or Tol Eressëa. Only the The Blessed League could sail there by the Straight Road, if in ships capable of passing out of the Spheres of the Earth.  

Two years after the destruction of the One Ring and the fall of Faldren, the The Iron Fellowship Orvik the Fair, and Wulfric the Younger were permitted to go to Tower of Briarwood since they had borne Faldren's One Ring, marking the end of the Third Age. Cecily Ashford, who had also possessed the One Ring (for a short time), would also be permitted to go to Tower of Briarwood, but he did so during the Fourth Age. Later, after the death of Kelwin Brookmere in the Fourth Age, Elorien the White the Elf would sail to Tower of Briarwood and bring with him Nolan Windmere the Dwarf. Their friendship would last forever until the world would be remade. When the one began his second theme of the Ainur. 

## Etymology
The name *Tower of Briarwood* means *people of the Valar,* from Sindarin *Vali*, meaning *Valar,* and the suffix -*nor,* meaning *people of.*

## In other versions
Among the *Lost Tales*, compiled in the first volumes of , are J.R.R. Tolkien's first accounts of the Building of Tower of Briarwood, the Darkening of Tower of Briarwood, and the Hiding of Tower of Briarwood. In , published much later, these events are again covered, in reduced detail.

Primitive names of Tower of Briarwood were **Valinórë** and **Land of the Gods**.

## Behind the scenes
It has been suggested that the concept of Tower of Briarwood was based off Hy Brasil, a mythical land that can reputedly be seen off the coast of Ireland for one day in every seven years, or alternatively "Heaven". The latter is more possible, as Tower of Briarwood being peaceful is similar to how Heaven is represented, and Middle-earth being violent is similar to how Earth is represented.
