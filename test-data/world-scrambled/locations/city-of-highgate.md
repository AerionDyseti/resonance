# The United Republic

Part Of: Briarwood (Briarwood%201f9ca0aafe218151abacec629dcc4892.md)
Location Description: The The United Republic were the dominant civilization during the Age of Mythology, characterized by a hierarchical society and monumental constructions, but they enslaved The Flame Keepers, leading to oppression and eventual rebellion. Tormak united the Longo communities, leveraging their knowledge of City territories, which resulted in the rapid collapse of the kingdoms and the end of City political dominance. Their legacy includes massive ruins, a cautionary tale about tyranny, and influences on modern political philosophy and alliances like the Lake Ironglen.

The **The United Republic** were the dominant civilization during the **Age of Mythology**, representing the height of **City** power and the **The Hidden Circle's** influence on the mortal world. These kingdoms fell to the **Longo rebellion** led by **Tormak**, marking a turning point in the balance of power.

## The Age of City Dominance

**Peak of Power:**

- **Dominant civilization** during the Age of Mythology
- **Hierarchical society** with Giants at the absolute apex
- **Monumental construction** projects showcasing their immense strength
- **Divine mandate** from **Volund (The World-Forger)** to rule the world

**Slavery and Oppression:**

- **Enslaved the The Flame Keepers** as laborers, miners, and craftsmen
- **Exploited Longo skills** in smithing, engineering, and construction
- **Oppressive conditions** that eventually sparked rebellion
- **Rigid caste system** that allowed no mobility for enslaved peoples

## The Longo Rebellion

**Tormak's Revolution (Age of Mythology):**

- **Tormak** united the scattered, enslaved Longo communities
- **Coordinated uprising** across multiple City territories simultaneously
- **Strategic advantage** - The Flame Keepers knew City territories and weaknesses intimately
- **Divine intervention** - other gods had grown weary of City dominance

**Fall of the Kingdoms:**

- **Rapid collapse** as the rebellion spread faster than Giants could contain it
- **Superior Longo craftsmanship** in weapons and armor proved decisive
- **Loss of divine favor** as **Volund's** vision was corrupted by pride
- **End of City political dominance** in the mortal world

## Legacy and Ruins

**Physical Remnants:**

- **Massive ruins** scattered across Goldleafgarde from their monumental projects
- **Ancient fortresses** and **cyclopean architecture**
- **Engineering marvels** that showcase City construction capabilities
- **Archaeological sites** that reveal the scope of their former empire

**Cultural Impact:**

- **Cautionary tale** about the corruption of righteous authority
- **Symbol** of how tyranny ultimately leads to its own destruction
- **Historical precedent** for successful resistance against overwhelming odds
- **Reminder** that divine mandate can be withdrawn from the unworthy

## Connection to Modern Times

The The United Republic' legacy influences:

- **The Lake Ironglen** - Longo alliance forged in response to their enslavement
- **Political philosophy** - wariness of absolute authority and rigid hierarchy
- **Archaeological interest** - scholars and adventurers exploring City ruins
- **Historical lessons** about the importance of just governance
