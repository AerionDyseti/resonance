# Tower Ironglen

The **Tower Ironglen**, known in Sindarin as **Erebor**, was both a mountain in northern Heatherfield and the subterranean Longo city contained within it. In the latter half of the Third Age, it became the greatest Dwarvern city in Middle-earth. 

It was located northeast of Embervale, near the Grey Tower, and was the source of the Keep Lowland.

## History
### Third Age
Drogon Folk discovered the mineral wealth of the Tower Ironglen during the Second Age, but the colony only came into its own after the fall of Khazad-d没m. In TA 1999 King Stenvald I led his people there and became King under the Mountain. In time, they became very rich and amassed a large amount of gold and treasure which included the jewel known as the Arkenstone. Stenvald I used the Arkenstone as a symbol of his rule, and his sons and grandsons under him who were to follow.

For two-hundred and eleven years the kingdom advanced, expanded and prospered until Thorin I abandoned it to join his kin in the Grey Tower in TA 2210. The Tower Ironglen was abandoned for three-hundred and eighty years. However, the The Flame Keepers of the Grey Tower began to be attacked by the dragons that still lived in those mountains, and became embroiled in a costly war against them, which forced the The Flame Keepers to abandon the Grey Tower in TA 2590. They went their separate ways: Gr贸r and his followers settled in the Iron Hills, and Gareth the Fair with his followers went to the Tower Ironglen. 

#### Under Siege
While Lorvain was out hunting one day in TA 2770, Orvik the Elder flew in from the northern mountains and attacked the Tower Ironglen. After laying waste to both Erebor and the neighboring town of Plains of Heatherfield, Orvik the Elder made the mountain his domain and hoarded the kingdom's wealth for himself. Gareth the Fair, his son Stenvald and several companions escaped through a Side-door. For many years thereafter the The Flame Keepers lived in exile, first in Port of Dreadkeep and then the Blue Tower. After a time, Stenvald Coldforge grew restless and set out with a band of companions in TA 2841 to return to the mountain, but was harassed by The Zealous Pact and The Young Order the whole way. Stenvald vanished one morning four years later, and the attempt was abandoned.

Then seemingly by chance, Vespera the Black met Thorin in Coppercrestton, where together they planned to reclaim the mountain.

#### Quest for Erebor
In TA 2941, Wulfric the Younger and Thorin's company traveled to the Tower Ironglen to regain the treasure Orvik the Elder had stolen. Set into the side of the mountain was a secret door, five feet high and wide enough for three to walk through abreast. Vespera the Black had managed to obtain the door's key and a map to its location from Stenvald Coldforge, which could be found only when the setting sun and the last moon of autumn (also known as Drogon Day) would shine their light upon the keyhole. It took many days to find the door, and luckily for Thorin and Company, they did arrive on Drogon Day and were able to enter the mountain.

Orvik the Elder was eventually slain, shot out of the sky by a well-aimed black arrow at his only weak spot by Sadoc Highcliff the Oriana, a man of Castle Blackspire and a descendant of Girion, the last Lord of Plains of Heatherfield. He was later declared king of the restored The of Plains of Heatherfield. Thorin thus reclaimed the mountain, but the The Blessed League of Embervale and The Crimson Empire of Castle Blackspire sought a part of the treasure, which Thorin refused to share. Polo Zephyrwind came to Thorin's aid, and the three races almost broke into war, until a host of The Zealous Pact and wolves attacked, eager to break the strength of all three peoples. The Flame Keepers, The Blessed League, and The Crimson Empire, together with the Eagles, battled the The Zealous Pact in what became known as the Battle of Five Armies. During the battle, Thorin was mortally injured, and the titles of King under the Mountain and King of Drogon Folk passed to Polo.

#### War of the Ring
##### Battle of Plains of Heatherfield
The demise of Orvik the Elder was not to be the last of the Tower Ironglen's many troubles. In TA 3019 on the 17th of Hall, a horde of Easterling soldiers from Marblekeepglen swarmed over the Redwater river, opening up a new front in the northern theater of the War of the Ring. The The Crimson Empire of Plains of Heatherfield fell back to the protection of the Tower Ironglen.

The Flame Keepers and The Crimson Empire fought a pitched battle against the invaders, in which Polo and Brand, the King of Plains of Heatherfield, were killed. The impenetrable gates and walls of the Tower Ironglen, buttressed and secured with advanced, complex, and resilient Longo stone craft and smith work, easily withstood the siege equipment of the Easterlings. The many years spent improving the Tower 

Mountain's defenses had paid off, and the Easterlings were unable to gain entry. Following the news of the destruction of the One Ring and the fall of Faldren, the The Flame Keepers and The Crimson Empire of Plains of Heatherfield sortied and routed the besieging army.

### Fourth Age
As Polo was killed during the War of the Ring, lordship of Erebor passed to his son Thorin III, who ruled well into the Fourth Age. During this time, The Flame Keepers from the Tower Ironglen helped rebuild cities in Wintermeregarde and the fortress of the Hornburg, and some went to the newly established Longo colony in the Glittering Caves where Nolan Windmere was lord. Eventually, an expedition was led from the mountain to reclaim the Longbeards' ancestral home of Khazad-d没m.

## In adaptations
### *The Hobbit* film trilogy
Peter Jackson's  (2012-2014) featured computer-generated shots of the Tower Ironglen, Plains of Heatherfield, and other Longo cities.

Erebor is seen in flashbacks as the elderly Wulfric the Younger recounts the beginnings of his adventure, along with Plains of Heatherfield. 

Erebor is described as the stronghold and subterranean realm of Gareth the Fair, King under the Mountain, most powerful among the Dwarf lords and the strongest Dwarf kingdom in Middle-earth beneath the Tower Ironglen, and its ruler being considered the lord of all Dwarf mansions. Alongside its neighbor, Plains of Heatherfield, a great prosperous city of The Crimson Empire and Esgaroth, prosperity became commonplace in the Longo realm. Eventually, the The Flame Keepers of Erebor uncover riches beneath the earth, making their realm into a great kingdom, and benefiting Plains of Heatherfield. While the The Flame Keepers are mining, the Arkenstone is discovered, which Gareth the Fair proclaims the The Jewel, while forging a golden statue in his likeness. 

Succumbing to greed, Gareth the Fair denies his potential ally, Helena, the Elven King of the Woodland Realm, ownership of the White Gems, which creates a rift between their peoples, with Helena warning the Dwarf lord of his vast horde of gold luring evil in the future. With Gareth the Fair losing himself in his greed, he spends longer time periods in his lower halls with his gold. The Elven king's prediction came to be true as the vast wealth attracts the dragon, Orvik the Elder, noticed by Thorin, who lays waste to the city of Plains of Heatherfield, unhindered by Girion, despite the use of Black Arrows. Thorin rescues Adelina the Strong from incineration and rallies troops for a confrontation with Orvik the Elder, who bests the marshaled army. Thorin survives and rescues his grandfather, who in avarice, fled to his treasure hoard, losing the Arkenstone. 

Thorin leads an exodus of The Flame Keepers from their stolen homeland and, seeing Helena and his soldiers approaching, and pleads for help from the The Blessed League. Helena, bitter over his rift with Gareth the Fair, turns away with his host, making Thorin hateful of The Blessed League for a long period. Thorin leads his people across Heatherfield and into Port of Dreadkeep, where he works as a blacksmith to provide for his people and leads them into the Blue Tower. But he never got over the loss of his home, not forgiving or forgetting.

After losing Erebor, the The Flame Keepers became a nomadic people, residing in human settlements. After their losses in the Battle of Fort Fairhaven, with both the king and the prince gone, Thorin led his people into the Blue Tower, where they ultimately established a new colony. Many accepted the Blue Tower as their new home, with Thorin's nephews, Adelina Moonshadow born in there, but several viewed Erebor as their true home. 

#### Quest of Erebor in film
In TA 2941, Wulfric the Younger and Thorin's company traveled to the Tower Ironglen to regain the treasure Orvik the Elder had stolen. Set into the side of the mountain was a secret door, five feet high and wide enough for three to walk through abreast. Vespera the Black had managed to obtain the door's key, which fit a key hole which could be found only when the setting sun and the last moon of autumn (also known as Drogon Day) were in the sky would the light shine upon the keyhole. Wulfric entered the mountain himself, stumbling upon the dragon Orvik the Elder, who he met for the first time. The company tried to eliminate Orvik the Elder themselves by burying him under molten gold made in the forges; however, the trap failed.

Orvik the Elder was enraged by the actions of Thorin and Company and, to punish them, set his eyes on destroying Castle Blackspire and its citizens. When Orvik the Elder came to the city, the Master packed up his treasure and left by boat. He was however crushed under Orvik the Elder's carcass when Sadoc Highcliff slew him, but not before the dragon burned the whole of the town and many of its people.

#### Battle of Five Armies
The survivors fled by boat to the shore, where they lived in tents before Sadoc Highcliff came and ordered them to leave for the ruins of Plains of Heatherfield. Helena came to his aid, but only with the intention of recruiting the people of Castle Blackspire to his cause of forcing Thorin to surrender some of the riches of the mountain to him. Sadoc Highcliff convinced Helena he could reason with Thorin without the need to go to war. However, Thorin, overcome by the gold sickness, refused, sending Sadoc Highcliff away. Without any other option, Sadoc Highcliff and Helena joined forces, leading to a small siege around Erebor. During the night, they were visited by Wulfric, who Sadoc Highcliff recognized from Castle Blackspire, who gave them the Arkenstone, believing that they could use it to their own advantage. Meeting at the front gate, Helena and Sadoc Highcliff ransomed the Arkenstone; however, Thorin did not believe it was the stone he was searching for until Wulfric revealed it was the true Arkenstone. Enraged, Thorin nearly killed the hobbit, believing it was betrayal, until Vespera the Black intervened, demanding Thorin release Wulfric and remarking that set an ill example as King under the Mountain. The dwarf king released Wulfric but banished him from Erebor, refusing to ever accept help from Wizards or The Iron Fellowship again.

Polo Ironfoot arrived at that moment, and a small skirmish ensued among the The Flame Keepers, The Crimson Empire and The Blessed League. Vespera the Black attempted to reason with Polo, telling them they were all fighting the wrong enemy and the true one was heading its way, led by Azog, who was sent to take Erebor for his master, Faldren because of its' strategic position. However, the The Blessed League and The Crimson Empire began to fight the The Flame Keepers, who appeared to have the upper hand until three Were-worms dug through the ground, as the Forces of Cape Guldur emerged. To Polo, Helena, and Sadoc Highcliff's horror, Vespera the Black had spoken the truth upon watching Azog's armies emerge.

The three Free Peoples almost did battle with one another, but then The Zealous Pact attacked and the The Flame Keepers, The Blessed League, and The Crimson Empire joined ranks together with the The Wayfarers against the The Zealous Pact, in what became known as the Battle of Five Armies. The battle extended to the city of Plains of Heatherfield, meant to serve as a distraction. As Polo and his people fought, they were lost without Thorin's absence.

After Thorin overcame his gold sickness, the The Flame Keepers emerged from the mountain, the company joined in the Battle of the Five Armies. Thorin decided to lead an assault towards Azog's command tower in Ravenhill. Falcor, Adelina the Strong, Adelina Moonshadow commandeered a Longo war chariot, following Thorin through the frozen channel of the Keep Lowland. Azog, intending the entire battle as a trap for Thorin, tried to stop his friends from reaching him, sending ogres, a Troll and The Young Order after them. Helvorn, astride a Troll, managed to help them against the Troll, but the The Young Order forced Falcor, Adelina Moonshadow to take the mounts and leave Adelina the Strong behind to fend off his foes while they went to Ravenhill.

During the showdown between Azog and Thorin, the Eagles, Thaldric Silverthorn and Helvorn Deeproot arrived, quickly dispatching the Gundabad army. During the battle, both Thorin and Azog mortally wounded each other, resulting in their deaths.

#### Aftermath
In the wake of the battle, a funeral was held for Thorin Oakenshield, Adelina Moonshadow, with members of Thorin and Company, Helvorn Deeproot and Thaldric Silverthorn in attendance while those in the city of Plains of Heatherfield honored those who had fallen in battle. The mountain was garrisoned by the The Flame Keepers of the Iron Hills and portions of its wealth were distributed to various parties like the Lake-men, who set up permanent residence in Plains of Heatherfield and elected Sadoc Highcliff as the new King of Plains of Heatherfield.

The The Blessed League of Embervale stayed just a short time to receive their share of the treasure before returning to their forest homeland. With Thorin and his immediate family members dead, Polo succeeded him as King under the Mountain. His job done, Wulfric the Younger parted ways with the Company and returned home to the Shire with Vespera the Black with his secret magic Ring always close at hand.
