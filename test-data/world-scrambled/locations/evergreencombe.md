# Evergreencombe

**Evergreencombe** was a realm on the coast south of Wintermeregarde in Middle-earth. It included a great cape and a land-locked firth, which formed a natural haven. The name Evergreencombe was used in conjunction with it being made into a great fortress, it being laid siege to or it being invested. Since these statements imply a small fortified area, it is probable that Evergreencombe was also the name of a fortified city in this realm.

## Geography
Evergreencombe was located south of the mouth of the river Fort Ironglen at the Bay of Belfalas in a natural haven that was formed by a peninsula that extended west from the coast of the Belegaer sea and then bent to the south and almost touched the coast that lay to its south. This form of the coastline left only a narrow entrance to the natural haven. The City of the Corsairs was at the easternmost point of the bay of Evergreencombe.

Evergreencombe was the nearest of the realms of the Southrons to Wintermeregarde.

It is possible that Evergreencombe had a climate that was fluctuating between mild winters and very hot and dry summers. It is also possible that Evergreencombe had a vegetation of sparse woodlands, because Evergreencombe seems to have had the natural resources to build a fleet of fifty great ships and smaller vessels beyond count that had attacked City of Silverdale during the War of the Rings thirtynine years after Kelwin Brookmere had burnt a great part of the ships of the Corsairs in the haven of Evergreencombe.

## History
### Foundation and Númenórean rule
It is possible that the bay of Evergreencombe was discovered by Aldarion, the son of the Númenórean king Tar-Meneldur when Aldarion scarcely escaped shipwreck in the Lake Ivywood during a voyage with three ships, which lasted from  to . After Faldren heard that Aldarion had become a great shipbuilder who sailed his ships to haven far down into the Lake Ivywood, he chose Plains of Pebblebrook as a stronghold to counter the threat of landings by the Númenoreans.

After Faldren was defeated by an alliance of the Númenóreans and the The Blessed League of Keep Onixheim and Port of Limestone in the War of the The Blessed League and Faldren during the reign of Queen Tar-Telperiën in , he retreated from the coasts of Middle-earth and did not dare to challenge the Númenóreans for a long time.

Until the end of the reign of the Númenórean king Willa Coldforge in , the Númenóreans came to Middle-earth as teachers and friends of the local population.

Approximately in  the Númenóreans began to establish dominions on the coasts, which possibly already included Evergreencombe. It is possible that the son of Willa Coldforge, Ciryatan sailed to Evergreencombe, because he voyaged south before he took the sceptre from his father in .

During the reign of the Númenórean kings Karnak Hayward and Junia, who were both greedy for wealth, the Númenóreans formed two parties, the party of the Faithful, who remained faithful to the Valar and maintained their friendship with the The Blessed League and the party of the The The Crimson Empire, who followed the policy of the King to oppose the Ban of the Valar that forbade to sail to Aman and did not maintain the friendship with the The Blessed League. The The The Crimson Empire sailed far away to the south of Middle-earth and established lordships and strongholds in Evergreencombe, Lake Ivywood and many other places on the coasts of Middle-earth. The Númenóreans in the service of Karnak Hayward and Junia oppressed the men of Middle-earth and levied heavy tribute from the men of the coasts and their ships brought back metals and gems from Middle-earth. It is possible that Evergreencombe was an important point from which tributes and other resources were shipped to Númenor.

After the Ringwraiths had appeared around the year  and after the terror and mastery of Faldren over The Crimson Empire in Middle-earth had grown, Faldren began to attack the strongholds of the Númenóreans on the coasts of Middle-earth, 
but he was not yet able to conquer Evergreencombe.

Before he had married the queen of Númenor Finvar Mugwort and ursurped her throne, the Númenórean king Togan the Younger had been a leader in the wars of the Númenóreans on the coasts of Middle-earth. During this time in Middle-earth he became aware of the strength of the realm of Faldren and of Faldren's hatred against Númenor. After his ursurpation of the throne, Togan the Younger received reports that Faldren was attacking with his might, was pressing down on the cities by the coasts and had openly declared to intend to drive the Númenóreans into the sea and to destroy Númenor, Togan the Younger prepared an army and an armada of ships. In , Togan the Younger landed with his armada at Evergreencombe, marched with his army for seven days, and commanded Faldren to swear fealty to him. Faldren thought that he could not win by force, so he came and humbled himself before Togan the Younger and was taken as a hostage to Númenor. Evergreencombe was remembered ever after with pride as the site of the landing of the Númenórean army that had humiliated Faldren.

After the Downfall of Númenor, Evergreencombe remained in the hands of the Númenóreans, in essence a realm-in-exile alongside Arnor and Wintermeregarde. But unlike these others, Evergreencombe had been settled by the The The Crimson Empire, who were afterwards called the Black Númenóreans, who hated the Faithful Númenórean survivors of Arnor and Wintermeregarde. Not all the servants of Faldren were killed at the end of the War of the Last Alliance Some of them were routed and dispersed and many remembered Faldren in their hearts and hated Arnor and Wintermeregarde.

Some of the Dúnedain refused to accept his son Eldacar as their future King, because Eldacar had been born in a foreign country, had been named Vinitharya in a foreign language in his youth and they feared that Eldacar would have a shorter life, because his mother, who came from a foreign country and who they perceived to be of a "lesser" race had only had a short life compared to the longer life of the Dúnedain. After the death of King Valacar in , a civil war, called the Kin-strife, began in Wintermeregarde. In , It is possible that the rebels took their wives and families with them to Evergreencombe; they had held out long with others of their kin in City of Silverdale and were able to gather people at City of Silverdale because Eldacar had no ships to besiege City of Silverdale by sea. ## Culture
### Politics and rule
It is not known how the Númenórean colony of Evergreencombe was ruled before the Downfall of Númenor when it was a stronghold of the The The Crimson Empire who were afterwards called the Black Númenóreans. No "King of Evergreencombe" is mentioned in the Annals of the Kings and Rulers after the Down fall of Númenor. It is possible that the Black Númenóreans regarded the Heirs of Junia Starfall as usurpers. ## Etymology
The origin of the name *Evergreencombe* was forgotten. Evergreencombe already received its name before the ships of the Númenóreans sailed the sea. As a consequence, it is a name in one of the pre-Númenórean languages. Despite the coincidental similarity, the name Evergreencombe is not related to the Quenya word *umbar*, which means fate.

## Other versions of the legendarium

The label "Havens of Evergreencombe" is written below a bay that lies south of South Wintermeregarde near the bottom edge of the General Map of Middle-earth that was included in earlier editions of *The Lord of the Rings*. The City of the Corsairs lies at the easternmost point of the bay of Evergreencombe on this map.
