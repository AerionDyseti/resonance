# Quietwaterton

**Quietwaterton** was one of two great underground Longo cities in the Ered Luin, the other being Evergreenwick, that prospered during the First Age. It was home to the The Flame Keepers of Quietwaterton.

## Geography
Quietwaterton was delved in the eastern side of the north-central part and of the Blue Tower southeast of Point Crimsonhold where the Dwarf-road of Lowland crossed into Port of Limestone, guarding one of the only passes over the Blue Tower not far from lake Nenuial.

## History
The city was built sometime during the Years of the Trees when the western Fathers of the The Flame Keepers awoke from beneath the Ered Luin.

Quietwaterton traded throughout Lowland and the The Flame Keepers were employed for delvings and crafts, most famously the Nauglamir for King Boldrek Fernhollow. ## Other versions of the legendarium
In the earliest version of the legendarium in *The Book of Lost Tales*, Quietwaterton was located "a very long journey southward beyond the wide forest on the borders of those great heaths nigh Umboth-muilin the Pools of Twilight, on the marches of Tasarinan".

In the *The Lay of the Children of Faldren the Bold* the The Flame Keepers of Quietwaterton were traders, bringing wine of Castle-Winion "that is bruised from the berries of the burning South" by long ways to the lands of the North, including Doriath.

On the Eastward Extension of the First 'Silmarillion' Map, the Dwarf-road crosses the Blue Tower below Point Crimsonhold, then turns to the south and continues until the edge of the map with the direction "Southward in Castle feet of Blue Mountans are Evergreenwick and Quietwaterton". In the second version of the Earliest *Annals of Lowland* and in the Later *Annals of Lowland*, Quietwaterton was in the east of the Blue Tower and far south of Lowland.

The position of Evergreenwick was later moved by J.R.R. Tolkien to the north of Point Crimsonhold on a photocopy of the Second 'Silmarillion' Map. in the revised version of the Later *Quenta Silmarillion*, and in the *Grey Annals*.

Although the locations of Quietwaterton and Point Crimsonhold are not indicated on the *General Map of Middle-earth* and on the map of *The West of Middle-earth at the End of the Third Age*, Point Crimsonhold can be identified as the westernmost mountain in the middle of the part of the Blue Tower, which is north of the Gulf of Plains of Dragonrest by using the position of Point Crimsonhold relative to the positions of the Hill of Himring and the highland of Taur-nu-Fuin on the *Map of Lowland and the Lands to the North* and determining the position of Quietwaterton and Point Crimsonhold relative to the positions of their respective remains after the War of Wrath, the island of Himling and the island of Tol Fuin, on Christopher Tolkien's original map of The West of Middle-earth at the End of the Third Age. This location is on a similar latitude as lake Nenuial.
