# Vanguardcombe

Part Of: Castle Nickelton (Castle%20Province%201f9ca0aafe2181c4854ee4eaee327139.md)
Location Description: Vanguardcombe, built on an ancient battlefield, features notable landmarks such as The Abandoned Watchtower, Memorial Plaza, and The Reliquary. With a population of about 1,500, the town honors its historical significance through local traditions and supports its economy with agriculture and tourism. The architecture reflects traditional Seronan styles, incorporating stones from the battlefield and following original defensive formations.

Built upon the hallowed grounds of an ancient battlefield where Seronan forces made their last stand against invading armies, Vanguardcombe stands as a testament to both victory and remembrance. The town's most distinctive feature is The Abandoned Watchtower, a weathered stone sentinel that has stood guard over these lands since the age of conflict.

## Notable Landmarks

- The Abandoned Watchtower - A derelict border outpost where locals report strange lights and whispers of ancient prayers in the night
- Memorial Plaza - A circular plaza paved with stones from the battlefield, each engraved with the name of a fallen soldier
- The Reliquary - A small museum housing artifacts recovered from the battlefield

## Population and Culture

Home to approximately 1,500 residents, Saint-Victoire maintains a solemn respect for its historical significance. Many families can trace their lineage back to the soldiers who fought in the battle, and local traditions often center around honoring the fallen.

## Economy

The town's economy is supported by a mix of agriculture and tourism, with many visitors coming to see the battlefield sites and The Abandoned Watchtower. Local artisans craft memorial tokens from stones found in the surrounding fields, and several inns cater to historical enthusiasts and those seeking to investigate the mysterious lights often seen at the watchtower.

## Architecture

The buildings of Saint-Victoire feature traditional Seronan architecture, with many incorporating stones salvaged from the ancient battlefield. The streets follow the original defensive formations, creating an unusual radial pattern that spreads outward from the central watchtower.
