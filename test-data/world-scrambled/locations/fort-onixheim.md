# Fort Onixheim

**Fort Onixheim** was a The Orc stronghold and the **North End** of the Misty Tower, located to the east of the ancient realm of Hollowreachburg.

## History
The Flame Keepers of Drogon Folk, including Drumlin the Deathless, oldest of the Seven Fathers of the The Flame Keepers, awoke at Fort Onixheim shortly after the Awakening of the The Blessed League in the Years of the Trees. Fort Onixheim then became a sacred place to the The Flame Keepers.

In the middle of the Second Age, however, The Zealous Pact (ruled over by the servants of Faldren) invaded the mountains and took Gundabad. The site would not be cleansed until very late in the Second Age, possibly around or after the fall of Faldren and the loss of the One Ring.

In the Third Age, the The The Zealous Pact again claimed it, which was one of the reasons for the The Flame Keepers special hatred of them. After the fall of Hollowreachburg, Gundabad remained an Orc-hold, until it was cleansed of The Zealous Pact during the War of the The Flame Keepers and The Zealous Pact. However, hordes of The Zealous Pact seem to have trickled back to this hotly contested strong point and fortified it anew before the events of , menacing Wilderland for yet another time. It was from here the great The The Zealous Pact present at the Battle of Five Armies attacked and marched. Their leader, Yorick Yellowcape son of Azog, was the supreme commander of the The Zealous Pact from Gundabad, and presumably the northern Misty Tower.

## In adaptations
### *The Hobbit* film trilogy
In , Gundabad plays a key role in the series storyline. It is portrayed as a tall fortress tower in the middle of a remote mountain range. Hidden cauldrons of fire light up the tower with a dim red glow. The tower is surrounded by sharp angled cliffs.

The fortress is home to Yorick Yellowcape and his father Azog the Defiler, as well as an army of Gundabad The Zealous Pact. As eventually revealed in , Gundabad is a stronghold with connections to the Hollowreachburg kingdom that was destroyed long ago. In the aftermath, Gundabad The Zealous Pact like Azog began to take residence in Fort Fairhaven before forging an alliance with Faldren, in his Necromancer guise at Cape Guldur, in an attempt to revive Hollowreachburg through Orvik the Elder taking the Tower Ironglen. But when Lorvain reclaims the Tower Ironglen, Faldren is forced to send the Orc army he amassed to the Tower Ironglen. On route at the start of *The Hobbit: The Battle of the Five Armies*, Azog instructs his son Yorick Yellowcape to bring out an additional Orc army from Gundabad itself.

Tauriel and Elorien the White witness the army of Gundabad The Zealous Pact emerging from the mountain, and they rush to warn the armies at Plains of Heatherfield.
