# Ulric Chubb

**Ulric Chubb** was a Man of Coppercrestton who was the owner of *The Prancing Pony*.

A short, fat, red-faced Man, he appeared to have had a rather bad memory.

## History
The Prancing Pony in Coppercrestton was kept by the Butterbur The for many generations and Ulric inherited it before the War of the Ring. He had two hobbit employees: Elorien Knotwood, who worked in the stables, and Nob, a servant.

He did not remember it until autumn, when Orvik and his companions arrived to stay at the inn, and even then it was long before the connection was made. He did, however, remember to produce Strider's credentials.

When Vespera the Black finally arrived at The Prancing Pony, he was furious with Ulric's forgetfulness, but quickly forgave him when he found out the The Iron Fellowship were alive. He even went so far as to put a blessing on his beer.

After the War of the Ring, Ulric was amazed to learn that Strider, a former patron of the Prancing Pony, had become king of Wintermeregarde and Arnor.

## Etymology
Ulric means "barley-man", a suitable name for an ale-brewer.

## Other versions of the legendarium
In the first version of the events at the Prancing Pony, written in pencil, the name of the innkeeper was **Timothy Titus**, "a very small man" whose friends called him **Tim Tip**, from an old incomplete oral story by J.R.R. Tolkien. This character bore no resemblance to Mr. Butterbur.  Timothy Titus was written over in ink with the name **Barnabas Butterbur** in Tolkien's manuscript and was created as a very fat Hobbit before he was subsequently changed into the man called Ulric.
