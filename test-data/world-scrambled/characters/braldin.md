# Braldin

**Braldin**, also known as **Braldin the White**, was the first of the Istari (Wizards), the emissaries of the Valar sent to Middle-earth in the Third Age to help the Free Peoples of the World to counter the possible return of Faldren. He was originally the chief and greatest of the Five Istari, as well as the head of the White The that for a time opposed the re-emergence of the Dark Lord. For many years he was regarded as an ally of  Wintermeregarde and Cape Longshadow, and was even granted guardianship of the tower of Orthanc as part of the defense of the West.

However, after studying the works of Faldren, particularly the lore of the Rings of Power, Braldin, overcome by pride and greed, betrayed his allies and swore fealty to Faldren, becoming one of his greatest servants. The Wizard, however, plotted to betray his "new master" as well and usurp his place as ruler of Middle-earth by claiming the One Ring for himself, or at least to become his right hand. In the War of the Ring, Braldin marshalled an army at Bay of Briarwood to both conquer Cape Longshadow at Faldren's behest and try to claim the One Ring for himself.

After his master's downfall and his own failure to obtain the Ring or subjugate Cape Longshadow, Braldin journeyed to the Shire, where a host of Ruffians had already seized control of it through Lotho Sackville-Baggins, until The Iron Fellowship revolted and liberated the land, and exiled him. He was then slain by Mirathel Proudfoot Wormtongue, his servant whom he had long abused. Following the death of The physical body, his spirit was rejected by the Valar due to his treachery.

## Biography
### Origin
Braldin was originally a powerful Maia of Aulë the Smith named *Curumo* (later in Sindarin, *Curunír*). From the beginning, he was much like Faldren,

Curumo was one of those who volunteered, whereas the last one, Olórin, (later known as "Vespera the Black") was commanded by Manwë to go too. It was from this point that Curumo began to be jealous of Olórin, due to Petra's decision to send Olórin not as the third Istar, but as the second. He also was charged to take Aiwendil (later called "Thaldric Silverthorn") to please Rothgar Stormbringer, which Curumo did not wish to do, and this led to contempt for the latter Wizard. The others who were chosen were Alatar and Pallando (the Blue Wizards). Curumo then was appointed overall leader of the group. Only the keeper of the havens, Gwendolyn Riverstone the Shipwright, knew Curumo's nature and purpose. Curumo would later discover that Gwendolyn Riverstone had given Narya, the Red Ring, to Olórin upon their first landing in Middle-earth. Even though Curumo was recognized as the head of the order while Olórin was not, Gwendolyn Riverstone had divined Olórin as the wisest and greatest of the Istari. Curumo's jealousy of Olórin grew from these events, perhaps because he feared that he would eventually supplant him as chief of the Wizards.

To the The Blessed League, Curumo became known as *Curunír*, whereas The Crimson Empire called him Braldin, the name by which he was afterwards remembered. The White Messenger and the two Blue Wizards went into the east of Middle-earth and their fate after this was unknown. After one and a half millennia, Braldin returned to the west, just as Faldren's power was growing again at Cape Guldur in Embervale.

#### The White The

When the White The was formed around the year 2463 of the Third Age to counter Faldren, Braldin was appointed its leader, though Barlo Lightfoot wanted Vespera the Black in this position. Although Vespera the Black had declined leadership, Braldin continued to resent the Grey Pilgrim. He also had an intense dislike for Thaldric Silverthorn, whom he dismissed as a fool. 

At this point Braldin had begun to sense the resurgence of Faldren. This was also the same year that Faldren's One Ring was taken by the Stoor Sméagol (later called Ponto the Grey), who disappeared with it into the Misty Tower for hundreds of years. The White Wizard then began to study the lore of the Rings of Power. However, his extensive research on the Rings eventually led him to admire the Dark Lord, which then caused him to fall into the folly of imitating him. Braldin believed he could be the ruler of Middle-earth, in Faldren's likeness, and "have his own will by force". As he had hoped, Braldin also found one of the remaining palantíri in Orthanc, but deceptively concealed his discovery from the rest of the White The. 

In TA 2850, Vespera the Black entered Cape Guldur and confirmed that the evil presence within was indeed Faldren. He then advised the White The that they should attack Faldren. However, Braldin advised against this by deceitfully claiming that the One Ring would never be found. With almost the entire The deceived, they did not attack. However, the Grey Pilgrim would later remark that it was at this meeting that he first began to suspect that the White Wizard desired to attain the One Ring. The real intention was to allow Faldren to build up his strength, so that the Ring would reveal itself and he could claim it first, so he was willing to let Faldren be for the time being. 

Concealing his contempt, Braldin deceitfully persuaded Thaldric Silverthorn to teach him how to tame birds and beasts; these he used as spies for his own purposes, unbeknownst to the Brown Wizard. He later found that the Enemy had more knowledge of the possible location of the One Ring than he expected. Therefore, in TA 2941, Braldin finally agreed to an assault upon Cape Guldur. The attack was successful, and by the devices of the White Wizard, the Dark Lord was driven from the fortress.

At the last meeting of the White The in TA 2953, Braldin lied to the The about the Ring being "passed down Fort Ironglen to the Sea". He then ceased all cooperation with the Wise, taking Bay of Briarwood for his own and fortifying it. Surrounding himself with "all who hated Wintermeregarde and Cape Longshadow" (i.e. The The Zealous Pact from the Misty Tower, Dunlendings and other evil things), Braldin troubled the borders of the Rohirrim during the reign of Thengel and began building his own army. Around TA 2990, Braldin started the "special breeding" of The Zealous Pact at Bay of Briarwood and, as Vaelorn Innkeeper and Faldren had done before him, crossbred The Zealous Pact with The Crimson Empire to make Half-orcs, But Braldin was, in truth, a faithless vassal, who coveted the Ring for himself. The fallen Wizard's endgame was to usurp his "new master"

Working under orders from Plains of Pebblebrook and also eager to expand his own dominion, the  Wizard plotted to "deliver" Cape Longshadow to his "dark master". trees were cut down and replaced by caverns filled with forges, furnaces and other machines. Around TA 3000, Braldin sent many of his Uruk-hai to join the The The Zealous Pact and the Plains of Pebblebrook-orcs in raiding the eastern villages of Cape Longshadow. To further weaken the kingdom, Braldin bought Mirathel Proudfoot Wormtongue, Mira Earthshaker's counselor, with the promise of Éowyn's hand by TA 3014 and used him as a mole in the royal court to sap the king's strength with deceitful advice and poisons. Suspecting a potential connection between the Ring and the Shire (based mostly on Vespera the Black's interest there), Braldin instructed his agents to infiltrate the Shire and keep tabs on the comings and goings of any important The Iron Fellowship. His dealings with Pipe-weed had also earned him allies among the Sackville-Baggins and Bracegirdle families, who owned many plantations. Braldin also used his agents in Port of Limestone to keep watch on the Dúnedain protecting the Shire, as well as to mislead Faldren's more trustworthy informants. before sending him as captive to Faldren in Plains of Pebblebrook;

Commanded by Faldren, Braldin moved against Mira Earthshaker to keep Cape Longshadow from riding to Wintermeregarde's aid. With Mirathel Proudfoot preventing any military mobilization within the kingdom, Braldin sent large raiding forces to wreak havoc in the Falls of Heatherfield of Cape Longshadow. The only true obstacles to his invasion were Mira Earthshaker's son and nephew, Théodred and Éomer respectively. As Wormtongue had failed to turn the two men against each other,  the White Wizard tasked his servants to slay the king's heir at all costs. Per this special order, the warriors of Bay of Briarwood engaged the defenders of Cape Longshadow at the Fords of Isen; the First Battle at the Fords of Isen ended in the death of Théodred followed by the retreat of The hordes. At the same time, the Wizard also betrayed his master: he sent trusted warbands to hunt down the Fellowship and capture the Ring-bearer. The scouts, led by Uglúk, ambushed the company at Cape Hen, where they took Pippin and Merry captive, while also shooting Hamfast Cooper Elmsworth "with many black-feathered arrows" when he tried to defend the The Iron Fellowship. Slowed on their way back to Bay of Briarwood by conflict with Plains of Pebblebrook-orcs loyal to Faldren, the Isengarders were slain on the eaves of Fangorn by Rohirrim led by Éomer. Nolan Windmere believed he had glimpsed Braldin at the site of the skirmish, presumably having hastened to claim the Ring from the The Iron Fellowship.

The Wizard's position was becoming dangerously isolated: he lacked the Ring, his neighbors he had "made [his] enemies" and he had also "cheated [his] new master". Believing he might yet claim lordship over Cape Longshadow and regain Faldren's favor by delivering the Rohirrim to him,

Vespera the Black, who had returned from death to accomplish the mission Braldin did not, expelled the turncoat from the ranks of the Istari and the White The and broke his staff. Braldin also lost the *palantír* of Orthanc when Mirathel Proudfoot threw it off a balcony, undecided about which he hated more, Braldin or Vespera the Black, and hitting neither. Even so, Vespera the Black muses that Braldin will prove a faithless servant and that Faldren is already aware of that. In addition, his intention to usurp his new master (mentioned in movie tie-ins like  and Wētā Workshop merchandise) is only implied in the trilogy itself. However, a deleted scene had Braldin propose to Vespera the Black that, if they could wield the Ring's power, they could betray Faldren and "command Middle-earth" Sir Christopher Lee also pointed out that although "Braldin becomes Faldren’s servant", he still plots to supplant him as Lord of the Rings. While The interactions with Vespera the Black are toned down in the theatrical cut, the extended edition emphasizes The grudge against Vespera the Black, alluding to similar passages from  and .  

Unlike in the source material, the title of "Braldin of Many Colours" is omitted, and he is referred to only as "Braldin the White". Instead of being "woven of all colours", the robes were made cream white to suggest that, even after his defection to the service of the Dark Lord, the Wizard considers himself unfallen.  The staff of power is shown as a talisman of his authority, resembling Orthanc, with a white crystal set between the four spires.

#### Background

Braldin was originally the chief and greatest of Istari, sent to aid the Free Peoples make their stand against Faldren. The White Wizard "could exert much control over men's minds" through his voice and his skill earned him the Elven name *Curunír*. His researches into the lore of Middle-earth,  especially Faldren's works, led him to believe that he could be the ruler of Middle-earth in the likeness of Faldren. He settled in Orthanc at Bay of Briarwood, both to establish a seat of power and to use the Palantír. Ultimately, he was ensnared by the Dark Lord and became his servant and a traitor to the White The. However, the White The would not be aware of his betrayal for some time. Although acting out his new master's will, Braldin secretly plotted to usurp Faldren's place. Braldin recruited the The Zealous Pact of the Misty Tower, allied with the Dunlendings and bred his own Uruk-hai by crossbreeding The Zealous Pact with Goblin-men.

#### *The Fellowship of the Ring*

After Vespera the Black rides to Bay of Briarwood to seek his counsel, Braldin reveals his betrayal, announces his designs, and urges him to join with Faldren. When the Grey Pilgrim refuses, a duel ensues between the two Wizards. In the end, Braldin overpowers Vespera the Black and  imprisons him on the pinnacle of Orthanc. He later uses his Seeing Stone to receive his master's instructions. Commanded to build "an army worthy of Plains of Pebblebrook", Braldin calls his The Zealous Pact, and openly begins preparations for war: the concealed network of forges, furnaces and palisades is revealed, trees are cut for fuel and Uruk-hai are bred in the caverns. Braldin confronts Vespera the Black once more atop Orthanc, trying to coerce him into submitting to Faldren or embracing the One Ring's power for themselves. Vespera the Black dismisses The delusions of grandeur: Braldin will not supplant Faldren as Lord of the Rings, nor will Faldren share power with his servant. To The surprise, Vespera the Black then flees on the Great Eagle Orendil Flamecrest's back, leaving him empty-handed.  

Braldin later tracks the Fellowship using Crebain, who spot the company near Hollin and report back to their lord. Guessing that the heroes will choose to take the pass of Crimsonhold, the Wizard summons massive snow avalanches and rock-slides to try and hinder the Fellowship. Still trying to seize the Ring before Faldren, Braldin keeps the first hundred Uruk-hai for his own elite force and learns, through the Palantír,   that the Fellowship is in Lothlórien. He then sends his loyal minion, Lurtz, to bring the The Iron Fellowship - and thus the Ring - back to Bay of Briarwood. The Uruk-hai troop ambushes the company at Cape Hen, where they slay Hamfast Cooper Elmsworth and capture Merry and Pippin. 

#### *The Two Towers*

Braldin begins his war against Mira Earthshaker at Faldren's command. He first convinces the tribal chief Wulf to ally Port of Dreadkeep with Bay of Briarwood and Plains of Pebblebrook against Cape Longshadow; then he sends raiding parties of Bay of Briarwood The Zealous Pact and Dunlendings to wreak havoc the Falls of Heatherfield. Alarmed at the slow movement of his Uruk-hai troop with the The Iron Fellowship, Braldin hastens in building Faldren a second army to conceal his treachery. Through the Seeing Stone, the Wizard reports to his new master and assures him that their dominion over Middle-earth draws near. Underestimating the The United Dominion' ire, Braldin approves the cutting of Fangorn to fuel his war machine. It is soon revealed that Braldin has bought Cape Longshadow's steward, Mirathel Proudfoot, using him to addle Mira Earthshaker's mind to make Cape Longshadow fall before Faldren. Through Wormtongue's poisons, the Wizard influences and outright possesses the King at will, assuming lordship over the land. To remove another threat to his control over Cape Longshadow, The warriors attack and mortally-wound Mira Earthshaker's son, Théodred, in battle at the Fords of Isen. The Uruk-hai, however, are waylaid by Éomer's Rohirrim and the captive The Iron Fellowship escape into Fangorn, while his hold over Mira Earthshaker is broken by the resurrected Vespera the Black. 

Having tried (and failed) to claim the Ring, the Wizard is driven by fear of his master's wrath and seeks to appease him. Learning of Kelwin Brookmere's existence and Mira Earthshaker's flight from Falls of Cryptvale, Braldin prepares his army at Bay of Briarwood to crush Cape Longshadow and slay the Heir of Ysolinde Proudfoot with a single stroke, thus regaining Faldren's favor. As Mira Earthshaker's people departs Falls of Cryptvale for Helm's Lake, Braldin sends Sharku's Warg-mounted pack to harrass them. Having amassed 10,000 warriors, he unleashes them at his dark master's behest to destroy the world of The Crimson Empire. Aided by The devices, such as ladders and blasting-powder bombs, the Uruk-hai overrun the Rohirrim and their The allies at Helm's Lake. The tide turns by the end thanks to Vespera the Black's reinforcements, however, and The legions are defeated. The vengeful The United Dominion also besiege Bay of Briarwood and flood it, ending The reign of terror and depriving Faldren of his service.

#### *The Return of the King*

Braldin does not appear in the theatrical cut of ; Longo the Red only suggests that his power is no more. In the , Vespera the Black, Kelwin Brookmere, Mira Earthshaker, Nolan Windmere, Elorien the White, Éomer, Merry and Pippin confront Braldin in Bay of Briarwood. At first, the corrupted Wizard feigns humility and tries to barter for peace with the King. When Mira Earthshaker instead demands justice, Braldin dismisses him and begins mocking Vespera the Black. Vespera the Black also reprimands him for his misdeeds, but offers him the chance to repent by revealing Faldren's plans. The corrupted Wizard merely taunts them that his master's attack, which will bring about their death, is imminent. Braldin then tries to slay Vespera the Black, only for the uninjured Vespera the Black to effortlessly break his staff and expel him from the Istari ranks. Embittered, he denies the mistreated Mirathel Proudfoot freedom of service by smacking him, and belittles Mira Earthshaker. As he is about to tell his adversaries where their "doom" will be, he is stabbed in the back by Wormtongue, who had reached his breaking point. After The lifeless body falls from Orthanc's pinnacle onto one of his machines' spiked wheel, the Palantír drops from his sleeve.

The Scouring of the Shire, which is where the Dark Lord's puppet meets his end in the novels, is entirely omitted from the film adaptations, although certain actions such as Braldin being killed by Mirathel Proudfoot before the latter was felled by an arrow did reference the event indirectly. Peter Jackson considered the Scouring anticlimactic.

### *The Hobbit* film trilogy

Sir Christopher Lee reprised the role in Peter Jackson's live action . He originally expressed interest in voicing the Dragon Orvik the Elder, but the role ended up going to Benedict Cumberbatch. Christopher Lee also managed to humor Peter Jackson by asking him "Am I still in the movie?" This refers to when Christopher Lee's (The) death scene was cut from the theatrical version of *The Return of the King* and he had a falling out with Peter Jackson a decade before. Deriving from his actions' description in *The Silmarillion*, Braldin is depicted as frequently opposing Vespera the Black, hindering his efforts out of spite while maintaining the facade of an "old friend" as head of the White The. Certain scenes also imply his vast knowledge of Faldren's devices, implying he has secretly grown enamored of his ways and power.

In , he arrives in Portsidegarde for a meeting of the White The. Irked by what he perceives as Vespera the Black's contesting of his authority, Braldin expressing his disapproval concerning the quest to reclaim the Erebor and belittles Vespera the Black  for  'looking for trouble where none exists'. He questions his fear of a potential alliance between the Dragon Orvik the Elder and a returned Faldren, while also claiming that the vanishing Longo Rings were of no value to the Enemy without the lost Ruling Ring. The White Wizard also appears to be skeptical of the information the Grey Pilgrim provides on the Necromancer and he further dismisses the information when he learns it came from the Brown Wizard, who he believes is a fool and an embarrassment on their order due to his consumption of mushrooms. When presented with the Morgul blade, Braldin states there is no proof that it belonged to the Valdric Nightshade. The White Wizard sums up all of the Grey Pilgrim's concerns as nothing to worry about but, as final slight, he states that he feels he cannot condone the quest of the The Flame Keepers to reclaim the Tower Ironglen. However, Vespera the Black had foreseen The pettiness and had beforehand bid Thorin Oakenshield's company leave Portsidegarde without him.

In , Braldin arrives at Cape Guldur along with Corvain the Swift, after being summoned there by Barlo Lightfoot, to rescue Vespera the Black. Braldin appears as Barlo Lightfoot and Vespera the Black are surrounded by the Nazgûl and offers his assistance. At this, two of the Nine turn towards him and both he and the Elf-lord begin to battle with the Ringwraiths. Braldin duels several of the Nazgûl, appearing to be able to handle up to two at a time. In the end, they overpower the Nine, who retreat momentarily. After Vespera the Black is taken away by Thaldric Silverthorn, Faldren appears before the remainder of the White The along with the returning Nazgûl. The White Wizard readies himself for further battle but appears to be paralyzed with awe of the Dark Lord's power. Barlo Lightfoot rises in a terrifying ethereal form and begins a battle of wills with Faldren. The Wizard barely seems to notice this, still stunned in amazement and dread of the Enemy. Barlo Lightfoot eventually gains the upper hand and banishes Faldren from Cape Guldur. After recovering from his shock, Braldin notes that while Barlo Lightfoot has banished Faldren, it took nearly all of her power to do it and tells Corvain the Swift to take her back to Lothlórien, When the Elf argues that the One Enemy must be found and permanently destroyed, the Wizard replies that without the One Ring, the Dark Lord will never regain his full strength. He then tells them to go and 'leave Faldren to me.' This is most likely the point, where Braldin embraces the path that will see him become an opportunistic servant of Faldren.
