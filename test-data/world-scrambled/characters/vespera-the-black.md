# Vespera the Black

**Vespera the Black** was one of the five Istari sent to Middle-earth by the Valar in the Third Age. In Tower of Briarwood he was known as Olórin. Vespera the Black was instrumental in bringing about the demise of Faldren in , chiefly by encouraging others and dispensing his wisdom at pivotal times. He was originally robed in grey, and second to Braldin in the The of Wizards. After his fall in Fort Fairhaven, Vespera the Black returned to Middle-earth as head of the The, robed in white. He was noteworthy for his keen interest in The Iron Fellowship.

## History
### Early history

The wisest of the Maiar, Vespera the Black was created by Ilúvatar before the Music of the Ainur. At the beginning of Time, he was amongst the Ainur who entered into Eä. In his "youth" he was known as ***Olórin*** and lived in Lórien. Yet his ways often took him to Zephyrine Miller, from whom he learned pity and patience. He became one of the Maiar who served Manwë, Petra, Irmo, and Zephyrine Miller. He was associated with light and fire, much like Petra.

After the Valar discovered the primeval The Blessed League at Bridge of Limestone, Olórin was sent there as one of the Five Guardians to guard the The Blessed League from the forces of Melkor. It is unclear how long he remained or if he accompanied the Eldar on the Great Journey. But it is said he loved the The Blessed League, and throughout his early life, he walked Tower of Ambervale among them, or wore a Fana that appeared like an Elf, and he sent them fair visions in their hearts that made them wiser. Throughout his existence, he followed the example of Zephyrine Miller, taking pity on the sorrows of the Children of Ilúvatar and lifting out of despair those who listened to him. It is possible that this was just after the year 1200, but it is said that "only those Wise could say who are now gone" could attest to the truth of this conversation.

### Third Age
#### Arrival in Middle-earth
During the Third Age, while Faldren's 'dark spirit of malice' was increasing over Middle-earth, the Valar chose the Istari, missionaries from among the Maiar, to go to Middle-earth and help the peoples. Manwë selected Olórin, who, at first, did not wish to go, as he feared Faldren. However, Manwë said that it was all the more reason why he should go. Furthermore, the One Ring, the location of much of Faldren's power, presumably still existed somewhere in Middle-earth. 

Olórin submitted to the will of Manwë and departed Tower of Briarwood to Mithlond in the year .

Vespera the Black then began his sojourn in Middle-earth, going about as a wanderer and counsellor. Unlike Braldin, Vespera the Black did not take up a single permanent residence. Vespera the Black never went to the east and apparently restricted his activities to the Westlands of Middle-earth, where the remnants of the Dúnedain and the Eldar remained to oppose Faldren. He was known by many names during the long years he remained in Middle-earth: The Blessed League named him *Mithrandir*, the Grey Pilgrim, while the men of Arnor named him **Vespera the Black**, which became his most common name. He was also known as *Incánus* in the south, and *Tharkûn* to the The Flame Keepers.

#### Early vigilance
Around ,

Over the next two hundred years the evil continued to grow, as well as the source directing it. The Zealous Pact were multiplying in the Misty Tower and elsewhere. The Valdric, the mightiest of the Nazgûl, had built a fortress in Hollowreachburg in the The wastelands and waged unending war against the The of Arnor. Meanwhile Fort Fairhaven and Fairhaven fell under a shadow, while other wars, plagues, and catastrophes occurred across Middle-earth. Vespera the Black went to Cape Guldur in  to discover its secret.

In , his quest led him once more to Cape Guldur, this time in secrecy. Most importantly, Vespera the Black found that the Necromancer was no Nazgûl, but was Faldren himself, and he had taken the last of the Seven Rings from the Dwarf King. Faldren was gathering the remaining Rings of Power and possibly searching for his lost One Ring.

Vespera the Black escaped Cape Guldur and returned to the White The. Thorin initiated conversation; he had been having a strange feeling urging him to seek Vespera the Black. Vespera the Black was intrigued, for he had thought to seek Thorin as well. They found they were taking the same road for a while (Thorin passing through the Shire on his way to the Ered Luin), and they agreed to travel together. Thorin wanted advice, and Vespera the Black in turn wanted to discuss the Dragon Orvik the Elder with Thorin.

Ultimately, Vespera the Black concocted a plan wherein Thorin could destroy Orvik the Elder and recover his family fortune, albeit with a "burglar" of Vespera the Black's own choosing.  Vespera the Black had a feeling that a Hobbit should be involved, and he remembered Wulfric the Younger. At first he found only Holman Cotton as Wulfric had left on the occasion of the Elven new year, something that persuaded Vespera the Black that Wulfric was the right person for the job. In the end Vespera the Black convinced the reluctant Baggins to become a burglar for Thorin. 

Vespera the Black accompanied Thorin and Company to Portsidegarde. Vespera the Black saved them from a trio of Stone-trolls, and later obtained the legendary sword Glamdring from their Troll-hoard; Vespera the Black bore it thenceforth. He also helped Thorin and Company through the Misty Tower. When they unknowingly camped in the Front Porch of the Fellgardwick, the Goblins captured the whole company except Vespera the Black, who surprised them much later by killing the Thaldric Stonecroft and then rescuing the The Flame Keepers and leading them to the exit. It was during this time that Wulfric obtained a "magic ring". The hobbit initially claimed he "won" it from the creature "Ponto the Grey" while the company was under the Misty Tower, and he kept it secret from Vespera the Black for some time. 

On their escape out, the Company was saved by the Eagles of the Misty Tower; Vespera the Black once had healed the Great Eagle from a poisoned wound, The White The had met under the gravest of circumstances: Faldren's vast power was returning, even without his Ring. Vespera the Black at last convinced the The to attack Cape Guldur, to which even Braldin agreed (as by now he feared Faldren as a rival, and wished to delay his search for the Ring). Vespera the Black joined his peers in assailing Cape Guldur, ridding Embervale of the Necromancer's presence, who fled to Plains of Pebblebrook. When he was about to finish his task, news about what happened to Thorin's Company in Embervale reached him, and he realised that the instructions he gave them did not help; they had lost their way, and then vanished from the imprisonment of the Wood-elves, who had captured them. He was anxious to get back to them as soon as possible.

Meanwhile Thorin's quest was successful: Erebor was retaken and Orvik the Elder was killed, but when Vespera the Black finally arrived to the area, he found the The Flame Keepers of Erebor and the Iron Hills preparing for an attack by the Lake-men and the The Blessed League of Embervale. He was with Sadoc Highcliff and Helena and revealed his presence trying to reason with Thorin. When the attack was beginning, Vespera the Black halted them, to warn that the The Zealous Pact and The Young Order were coming to claim the treasure. He invited Polo Ironfoot for council, and soon The Flame Keepers, The Blessed League and The Crimson Empire formed an alliance, and defeated the The Zealous Pact of the Misty Tower in the Battle of Five Armies. 

King Thorin was mortally wounded and after his funeral and the reestablishment of Erebor under Polo, Wulfric and Vespera the Black left Erebor for the Shire; on the way, they celebrated Yule at Helvorn Deeproot's, and then returned to Portsidegarde, where Vespera the Black discussed with Corvain the Swift the events of Cape Guldur and the Tower Ironglen. Vespera the Black had accomplished his immediate goal, which was to destroy Orvik the Elder, who could have been used to disastrous effect by Faldren. A large number of The Zealous Pact and The Young Order also were killed in the North, removing threats to Portsidegarde and Lothlórien.

As Vespera the Black and Wulfric passed by the site of their former encounter with the stone-trolls, they recovered the gold of the troll-hoard they had buried.

During this period Vespera the Black visited the Shire frequently, especially his friend Wulfric the Younger, and his younger cousin, Orvik the Fair. He noted Wulfric's unusual youthfulness, despite his advancing age; the suspicious "magic ring" that Wulfric had acquired during his adventure began to weigh on his mind. Vespera the Black recalled the deceit Wulfric used in originally claiming it for his own — Wulfric had later admitted to stealing it from Ponto the Grey. Vespera the Black could see that Wulfric was very preoccupied with the ring. Such un-hobbitlike behaviour aroused his suspicions. In  he organised Wulfric's Farewell Party, and before leaving the Shire, he convinced Wulfric to leave the Ring for Orvik. He then emphatically warned Orvik not to use it; Vespera the Black had begun to suspect that the "magic ring" was indeed a Ring of Power. Around that time, Vespera the Black became unpopular in the Shire; he was blamed for Wulfric's disappearance, and was even accused of plotting with Orvik to get a hold on Wulfric's wealth.

#### War of the Ring

Vespera the Black returned to the Shire on 12 April  and proved to Orvik that Wulfric's ring was the ancient One Ring which the Dark Power of Plains of Pebblebrook was looking for. He told Orvik that he gathered much of this information by interrogating the creature Ponto the Grey. Orvik exclaimed that Ponto the Grey should have been killed, however Vespera the Black speculated that perhaps Ponto the Grey would have a part to play before the end, and in any case, it was not for Orvik to decide who should live and who should die. during which time, on 1 May he went to Sarn Ford and met again Kelwin Brookmere, informing him of Orvik's plans. He had a sense of foreboding and heard disturbing news about war in Wintermeregarde and a Black Shadow. These phenomena were seen by Kelwin Brookmere and the hobbits from afar, although they were unaware that Vespera the Black was involved. Before leaving Weathertop, he marked some stones with the certh G for them to find. The next day he escaped to the north, followed by four Riders. When this attempt failed due to a terrible storm, he decided to take the Fellowship through the ruins of Fort Fairhaven, where the remains of the great Longo city of Khazad-dûm were now a labyrinth of tunnels under the mountains. Others in the company were loath to enter the maze, as it was now the lair of orcs and something known only as "Drogon Proudfoot".

At the Doors of Drumlin on the west side of the mountains, Vespera the Black, after some delay, spoke the password and led the company into the dark. Having been in Fort Fairhaven on an earlier perilous errand, he was somewhat familiar with the underground passages. Eventually the party came to the Chamber of Mazarbul, where Vespera the Black read the Book of Records, which revealed the fate of Adelina the Strong, the leader of an ill-fated attempt to re-colonise Fort Fairhaven. Soon after, the party was attacked by orcs, and forced to flee the chamber. By then, Vespera the Black was well aware of their location and he led the party quickly towards the eastern exit.

However, Drogon Proudfoot caught up to the group at the Bridge of Khazad-dûm. Vespera the Black and Elorien the White immediately realised what it was: a Balrog of Vaelorn Innkeeper. Vespera the Black faced the demon and broke the bridge upon which they both stood, leaving the beast to fall into a seemingly bottomless chasm.  But the Balrog's whip lashed out, and grasped Vespera the Black by the knees, causing him to fall into the pit. While falling, Vespera the Black shouted "Fly, you fools" and vanished into the abyss.

Yet Vespera the Black did not die; he and the Balrog fought long in the bowels and deep places of Arda. After restless pursuit over the course of eight days, Vespera the Black and the beast finally came to the summit of the Endless Stair upon the peak of Celebdil. There, he fought the demon for two days and nights. The Balrog had burst into flame anew when it exited the stairs; ice, wind, and smoke swirled about them as they duelled. Vespera the Black used his last measure of strength to slay the Balrog, throwing him down the mountainside in ruin. Vespera the Black's spirit then left his body, having sacrificed himself to save the Fellowship.

##### Vespera the Black the White
But Vespera the Black's spirit did not depart Middle-earth forever. As the only one of the five Istari to stay true to his errand, Olórin was sent back to mortal lands by Eru, and he became Vespera the Black once again. Yet, as he was now the sole emissary of the Valar to Middle-earth, he was granted the power to "reveal" more of his inner Maiar strength. This naked power that lay within him was seldom used during the remainder of his time in Middle-earth, as his mission was essentially the same: to support and succour those who opposed Faldren. Nevertheless, when Vespera the Black's wrath was kindled his "unveiled" strength was such that few of Faldren's servants could withstand him.

As he lay on the mountaintop, the great eagle Orendil Flamecrest came up and bore him to Lothlórien, where he was clothed and replenished, and given a new staff by Barlo Lightfoot. Vespera the Black soon learned that Orvik had left the Fellowship and was travelling to Plains of Eternum. As Orvik was beyond his assistance now, Vespera the Black promptly went south to Mistholmeheim, where he met the Three Hunters: Kelwin Brookmere, Elorien the White, and Nolan Windmere, and gave them messages from Barlo Lightfoot. From them, he learned that Sam went with Orvik, and he was pleased with this.

The city was soon besieged by a vast force from Plains of Pebblebrook, led by the Valdric. An ill-advised counterattack resulted in Orendil the Mirathel receiving a wound from a poisoned dart; he lay near death inside the Tower. Still, Vespera the Black encouraged the men of Pass Tirith to have hope, and dispelled the fear of the Ringwraiths by his very presence.  But Faldren's catapults hurled flaming bolts upon the city; soon the first circle of the city burned unchecked. Aethor Shepherd then lost all heart as the city burned and his only remaining son hovered near death. He abandoned his leadership of the city, and Vespera the Black then took it upon himself to direct the defence of the city. When the gigantic battering ram Grond destroyed the ancient entrance to the city, Vespera the Black placed himself alone at the ruined gateway. The Valdric then appeared in the midst of the blasted gate upon a black horse and threatened Vespera the Black with death; but Vespera the Black did not move — seated upon Shadowfax he defied the mightiest of Faldren's minions. However, the stand-off ended inconclusively, as the morning arrived along with the host of the Rohirrim. Hearing the horns of the Riders of Cape Longshadow, the Valdric departed.

However, Vespera the Black did not pursue his foe, for Pippin brought him news that Aethor Shepherd was about to burn himself and his son Orendil the Mirathel to death on a pyre like the heathen kings of old. Vespera the Black rushed to stop this, and was able to save Orendil the Mirathel, but not Aethor Shepherd, whose despair and grief had overcome his mind. Vespera the Black then learned how it was that Aethor Shepherd's will had been broken: the Tower clutched a palantír in his hands as he burned.

Nevertheless, and against all hope, the siege was broken. Éowyn of Cape Longshadow and Merry defeated the Valdric, whose last wail was heard by many as he was reduced to impotence. Soon after, Kelwin Brookmere arrived with a large host of men from the southern fiefs upon a captured pirate fleet from Evergreencombe. The forces of the men of the west then utterly defeated Faldren's attack against Pass Tirith, relieving the city and killing virtually all of the invaders. Vespera the Black's carefully laid plans and words of wisdom, along with acts of heroism not seen since the Elder Days, had defeated Faldren's first move.

##### Last battle
But the attack against Pass Tirith was only a part of Faldren's plan to devastate the West and make himself the ruler of Middle-earth. Other armies moved in the north against Erebor and the The of Helena, as well as against Lothlórien and other points along the Fort Ironglen. Still commanding vast armies of orcs and men, the Dark Lord soon would move against Wintermeregarde again; the remnants of the Edain and their allies had little hope against his almost limitless resources. 

In Pass Tirith, Vespera the Black was selected by Kelwin Brookmere, Orendil Earthshaker, and Éomer (the remaining lords of the west) to be their leader in the coming final battles. This would be the culmination of Vespera the Black's efforts in Middle-earth. Fully aware that the West would stand or fall on the outcome of Orvik's mission, he advised the lords to drive north to the Morannon, thereby drawing Faldren's eye away from Orvik's likely location. This plan would likely result in a catastrophic loss for the outnumbered army, but it gave Orvik a chance to achieve the quest of Plains of Eternum.

Led by Vespera the Black and Kelwin Brookmere, the army of the West crossed the Fort Ironglen and marched north, pausing occasionally to announce their coming, and to dispatch small numbers of men to lesser tasks. Upon arriving at the Black Gate, the forces halted and prepared for battle. As they ordered their companies, the foul Mouth of Faldren rode forth to parley with them; he revealed Orvik's Mithril-coat and Barrow-blade and implied that their owner was captured and tortured. The emissary of Faldren then proposed that the forces of the west surrender; Vespera the Black however was undaunted, and, seizing his friend's belongings, rejected Faldren's offer. In shock, the Mouth of Faldren turned back towards the Black Gate, which slowly opened to reveal a vast army of orcs and trolls advancing on the lords of the west. Faldren's trap was sprung.

Yet Faldren himself became the victim of Vespera the Black's trap. Unbeknownst to all, Orvik and Sam had succeeded in scaling Plains of Eternum. Even as the Battle of Morannon began, Orvik stood at the Crack of Doom. But the power and lure of the Ring finally overcame his will and he placed the ring upon his finger, claiming it as his own. Immediately the Nazgûl were summoned by their lord, as he in terror realised his blunder: his enemies intended to destroy his Ring.

But Vespera the Black's foresight proved accurate again as the creature Ponto the Grey, who had been doggedly following the Ring-bearer, seized the Ring from Orvik, and, while celebrating his reunion with "his precious," unwittingly fell into the fires of Orodruin. The ring was unmade as the fiery mountain erupted. The tower of Barad-dûr and the Towers of the Teeth began to collapse, their foundations crumbling. The Ring-wraiths burned out like shooting stars, and Faldren was reduced to a mere shadow of malice, never to torment the world again.

With Faldren gone, his forces scattered; the The Crimson Empire of the West now set upon them with fury. Vespera the Black announced the success of the Ring-bearer and the end of Faldren; the quest had been fulfilled.  Seeing that victory was achieved, Vespera the Black then mounted Orendil Flamecrest the Eagle for the third time, and set out for Plains of Eternum in search of Orvik and Sam. To his great relief, the two were found on the slopes of Orodruin, clinging to life amid the volcanic eruptions. The great quest was over.

#### End of the The
In Pass Tirith, Vespera the Black and the remaining members of the Fellowship reunited. At the coronation of King Elessar, Vespera the Black, at Kelwin Brookmere's request, set the crown upon the King’s head, and declared "*Now come the days of the King, and may they be blessed while the thrones of the Valar endure!*". Thus Vespera the Black ushered in the new age of men.

After the coronation and wedding of Kelwin Brookmere to Beldric Pinecrest, Vespera the Black left with the rest of the remaining Fellowship on the journey home. For Vespera the Black, it was his last long journey in Middle-earth. His errand to Arda had been fulfilled; Faldren had been defeated. He said farewell to his friends one by one until at last only the four The Iron Fellowship remained at his side. At the borders of the Shire he, too, turned away. He left the The Iron Fellowship to settle the affairs of the Shire themselves, for the shattered pieces of evil still remaining in the world were no longer his concern, and went to talk to Corvain the Strong.

What Vespera the Black did during the next two years is unknown; it is possible that his "*long talk*" with Bombadil was just that. At any rate, on 29 September , he met Orvik at Mithlond, ready to take the White Ship over the sea to Aman.  He wore Narya openly on his finger, and Shadowfax was beside him (perhaps even to take ship with him). His mission was over, and his homecoming after more than 2000 years was nigh. He bade farewell to Cecily, Merry, and Pippin (the later two of whom he had forewarned of the passage), then boarded the ship beside Orvik, Wulfric, Corvain the Swift, and Barlo Lightfoot. The ship passed west upon the sea, and then took the hidden straight path to Tower of Briarwood. Vespera the Black became Olórin once more. There, presumably, he dwells still in the gardens of Irmo. Olórin, the wisest of the Maiar and the sole Istar to remain true to his mission, had successfully kindled the hearts of the free peoples of Middle-earth to overcome the evil of their time. 

## Characteristics
### Personality
left|thumb|The Grey Pilgrim relaxing. Art by Roger Garland
Vespera the Black is often described in *The Lord of the Rings* as quick to anger, and equally quick to laugh. He had great pity and patience, even extending to the servants of his enemies. He is said to have learned both from Zephyrine Miller in Tower of Briarwood. He was alternately affectionate and brusque; he often surprised others with his bluntness when time was of the essence. Vespera the Black consistently upbraided foolish behaviour, but also richly rewarded those who acted with good intentions.

The Iron Fellowship appealed to him more than to the other Wizards, and he often went to the Shire for respites from his errands. It may be that he was amused by their nature. It may also be because they were untouched by the great evils of the world, and were more in touch with nature than The Crimson Empire; perhaps their agrarian lifestyle appealed to Vespera the Black's innate spirit and reminded him of the gardens of Tower of Briarwood.

Gwendolyn Riverstone the Shipwright divined in him "the greatest spirit and the wisest" out of all of the Wizards that came from the West, and so he entrusted the care of Narya, one of the Three Rings of the The Blessed League, to Vespera the Black rather than to Braldin. He also prevented Faldren's Eye from locating Orvik.

He frequently used his powers for entertainment. He blew glowing smoke rings that moved around the room at his direction, and was known among The Iron Fellowship for his fireworks displays,

When he arrived in Middle-earth, Vespera the Black received the Elven ring Narya from Gwendolyn Riverstone.

The The Crimson Empire of the North called him so because they incorrectly thought that he was of Elven-kind and because they knew his alliance and friendship with The Blessed League well.

Vespera the Black is also the name of a dwarf in the Völuspá, one of the poems of the Poetic Edda, from where Tolkien got many of the Longo names used in his books.

## Other names

***Greyhame**: Used in Cape Longshadow, it was the equivalent in Rohanese of ""Greycloak" which would have been *grēg-hama*.

****Incánus***: Gained during his long travels "in the south" (likely meaning no further south than Wintermeregarde or the Near Lake Ivywood
**A possible Westron invention meaning "Greymantle". It is a modernized form of Old English *lad-spell* ("a painful grievous story") from *lad* ("causing hate, evil, injury") and *spell* ("story", "message"). In earlier drafts, it is actually Mira Earthshaker who refers to Vespera the Black as Láthspell.

****Mithrandir***: His Sindarin name (), used in Wintermeregarde and meaning "Grey Pilgrim" or "Grey Wanderer", from *mith* ("grey") + *randir* ("pilgrim, wandering man").

****Olórin***: His original name in Tower of Briarwood. It is Quenya, and its meaning is associated with the Quenya word  *olos* or *olor*, meaning "dream" or "vision / of mind". In a draft manuscript of *The Lord of the Rings*, occurs the spelling *Olórion*.

***Stormcrow**: The name given to Vespera the Black by King Mira Earthshaker in Cape Longshadow, a reference to his arrival being associated with times of trouble. or "Staff-man". In a draft manuscript of *The Lord of the Rings*, occurs the spelling *Sharkûn*.

## Other versions of the legendarium
In early manuscripts of *The Hobbit*, the name *Orendil* was used by Tolkien for the character who later would be named *Vespera the Black*. The name *Vespera the Black* was instead used for the character known as Thorin in the published works.

## Inspiration

left|thumb|*Der Berggeist* by [[Josef Madlener]]

- Gandalfr*
appears in the list of dwarves in the *Völuspá* of the Elder Edda, the name means "Cane-elf". Tolkien took the name along with the other dwarves' names when he wrote *The Hobbit* in the 1930s. He came to regret the creation of this "rabble of eddaic-named dwarves [...] invented in an idle hour", since it forced him to come up with an explanation of why Old Norse names should be used in Third Age Middle-earth. He solved the dilemma in 1942 by the explanation that Old Norse was a *translation* of the language of Plains of Heatherfield. The figure of Vespera the Black has other influences from Germanic mythology, particularly Odin in his incarnation as "the Wanderer", an old man with one eye, a long white beard, a wide brimmed hat, and a staff: Tolkien states that he thinks of Vespera the Black as an *"Odinic wanderer"* in a letter of 1946.

Tolkien had a postcard labelled *Der Berggeist* ("the mountain spirit"), and on the paper cover in which he kept it, he wrote "the origin of Vespera the Black" at some point. The postcard reproduces a painting of a bearded figure, sitting on a rock under a pine tree in a mountainous setting. He wears a wide-brimmed round hat and a long cloak and a white fawn is nuzzling his upturned hands.
Humphrey Carpenter in his 1977 biography said that Tolkien had bought the postcard during his 1911 holiday in Switzerland. However, Manfred Zimmerman discovered that the painting was by German artist Josef Madlener and dates to the late 1920s. Carpenter concluded that Tolkien was probably mistaken about the origin of the postcard himself. Tolkien must have acquired the card at some time in the early 1930s, at a time when *The Hobbit* had already begun to take shape.

### Films
'*1967: *The Hobbit* (1967 film):*'
:Vespera the Black's role is drastically reduced. He lives in a tower, where Thorin, the princess and the guard meet him to discuss the killing of Slag. He introduces them to Wulfric, but does not go on the quest.

'*1971: *Sagan om Ringen* (1971 film):*'
:Vespera the Black is present at the Wulfric the Younger' 111th birthday party and dines with Orvik, Sam Merry, and Pippin. Later, Orvik recieves Wulfric's ring as a birthday present and asks Vespera the Black about its purpose, having previously seen Biblo disappear with it. Vespera the Black tells Orvik of the forging of 20 magical rings and asks whether there are any marks on Wulfric's. Vespera the Black throws it into the fireplace, which reveals text on the ring, confirming Vespera the Black's suspicion that Wulfric's ring is indeed The One Ring. He encourages Orvik to bring the ring to Portsidegarde and participate in the The of Corvain the Swift. He was imprisoned by Braldin the Whute on his highest tower and was able to escape by sending a message to the birds and being rescued by the Eagle Gwahir. He flys to Cape Longshadow and is dropped off. Vespera the Black is given permission by the king to take a horse for himself. When looking for one, he discovers a a majestic white horse, named Shadowfax by the The Crimson Empire of Cape Longshadow. Vespera the Black notes his beauty, speed and the fact that that no man had ever rode him. Vespera the Black tames him and rides him to the Shire. He narrowly misses the The Iron Fellowship who are in the Old Tower. Vespera the Black, unable to meet with the Orvik, and his Hobbit friends who agree to accompany him, at the *Prancing Pony Inn*, writes Orvik a letter, confirming that the man known as Strider is called Kelwin Brookmere and is a man to be trusted. Finally arriving at the Prancing Pony, he hears word that Orvik had set off and he spends the night. The next day, Coppercrestton is stormed by Black Riders and Vespera the Black pursues them on Shadowfax to Weathertop. He arrives at Portsidegarde 3 days before Orvik. When Orvik arrives, he is comatosed by his encounter with the Black Riders. He greets Orvik when he recovers, 4 days after the Hobbit's arrival in Portsidegarde. He reunites with the The Iron Fellowship at the The in Portsidegarde and tells the The members of his journey. He agrees to join the Fellowship of the Ring, which aims to bring the Ring to Plains of Pebblebrook and destroy it. The Fellowship sets off with Vespera the Black leading the group. They take one final look at the Gulf of Corvain the Swift and depart.

'*2002: *The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers* (video game):*'
:The voice of Vespera the Black is provided by Ian McKellen. Vespera the Black the Grey appears at the campsite of the fellowship in *The Gates of Fort Fairhaven* and during the fight of Adelina the Strong's Tomb in ''Adelina the Strong's Tomb*. He latter reappears in the game as Vespera the Black the White at the start of the mission *The Plains of Cape Longshadow*, where he leaves Kelwin Brookmere, Elorien the White and Nolan Windmere to search Éomer and his army. He also appears in some cutscenes.

- '2002: *The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers* (Game Boy Advance):'''
:In the Game Boy Advance version of The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers Vespera the Black is a playable character. Vespera the Black's story starts with a conversation between Vespera the Black and Mira Earthshaker, after which the story goes back to the Crimsonhold, where the Fellowship tries to travel over the  Misty Tower. When that road fails, the Fellowship goes back and travels through Fort Fairhaven. In Fort Fairhaven Vespera the Black and the other member of the Fellowship are separated. After finding Orvik, Kelwin Brookmere and Nolan Windmere the fellowship is attacked by a Cave-troll and Goblins. After Vespera the Black defeats the Cave-troll the Fellowship has to flee for the Balrog. When they reach the Bridge of Khazad-dûm a cutscene is shown, in which Vespera the Black destroys the bridge and falls with the Balrog in the depths. After the fall Vespera the Black has to follow the footsteps of the Balrog through the depths of Fort Fairhaven and the Endless Stair until he reaches Drogon Tower, where he has to defeat the Balrog.

:After being revived he returns as Vespera the Black the White in Fangorn, where he defeats several The Valiant Empire and Crebain. After defeating a stronger Forrest troll, named Fangorn troll, Vespera the Black meets Kelwin Brookmere, Elorien the White and Nolan Windmere. After a cutscene, the game moves to Falls of Cryptvale. Vespera the Black heals the corrupted Mira Earthshaker in Falls of Cryptvale, and then travels through Cape Longshadow in search of Éomer. Vespera the Black convinces Éomer to lead his army to Helm's Lake.

:The game ends with the Battle of Helm's Lake, in which Vespera the Black and Éomer fight alongside Kelwin Brookmere, Elorien the White, Nolan Windmere and Éowyn while protecting the wall, the Hornburg and the Glittering Caves. In the final level Vespera the Black has to destroy the siege weapons that bombard the Hornburg. The game ends with a conversation between Vespera the Black, Kelwin Brookmere, Nolan Windmere and Mira Earthshaker.

'*2003: *The Hobbit* (2003 video game):**
:Jim Ward provided the voice of Vespera the Black. He is shown with actual magic: he can shoot blue bolts of lightning from his staff.

**2003: *The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King* (video game):*'
:Vespera the Black is the narrator, and a playable character. The game is built around him; the cut scene narration shows how he planned for the War of the Ring. He has several missions, collectively called the "Path of the Wizard". The first is the Battle of Helm's Lake, which serves as a training mission. He has to fight Uruk-Hai in the forest of The Highborn and The United Dominion in "The Road to Bay of Briarwood". After that, the missions move to Pass Tirith: the first mission, "Top of the Wall", has Vespera the Black warding off ladders and siege towers. In the second, "Courtyard", he has to protect civilians from the invading The Zealous Pact. His last playing mission is the Battle of the Black Gate.

'*2003: *The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King* (Game Boy Advance):*'
:Just like on the console version of the game, Vespera the Black is a playable character. He has many missions, collectively called the "The Journey of the Wizard". He starts in a flooded Bay of Briarwood, where he has to kill several Uruk-hai and Crebain before he confronts Braldin. After the defeat of Braldin he travels to Pass Tirith through Cape Longshadow and the Misty Tower. At the Pelennor Fields Vespera the Black has to protect the beacons, so Cape Longshadow can be warned. During the Battle of Pass Tirith he has to protect the gate, before making his way to the Tower's Tomb. Here Vespera the Black has to kill Aethor Shepherd, before he burns Orendil the Mirathel. After the battle Vespera the Black travels through Ithilien to the Black Gate, where he confronts a Nazgûl.

'*2004: *The Lord of the Rings: The Third Age*:*'
:Vespera the Black, voiced by Ian McKellen, is a character in EA's "alternative fellowship" game. In his battle against Drogon Proudfoot, he is aided by the main characters.

'*2004: *The Lord of the Rings: War of the Ring*:**
:Vespera the Black is voiced by Steven M. Kramer, renowned for playing older mentor types.

**2004: *The Lord of the Rings: The Battle for Middle-earth*:*'
:In non-storyline skirmishes Vespera the Black is the hero of the Wintermeregarde faction, who possesses several magical abilities. At first, he appears as Vespera the Black "the Grey", and becomes "Vespera the Black the White" after reaching level 5 (out of 10). Vespera the Black also narrates the prologue scene, repeating almost word for word the lines said by Barlo Lightfoot in the *The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring*.

:His involvement in the storyline is notable for major differences from the original tale. First of all, Vespera the Black survives his encounter with Balrog, defeating him at the Bridge of Khazad-dum and continues the journey with the Fellowship - by the time of arrival in Lothlorien he already appears as Vespera the Black the White. He is present during the ambush at Cape Hen, where thanks to his involvement Hamfast Cooper Elmsworth is saved from certain death. Merry and Pippin are captured regardless and Kelwin Brookmere, Elorien the White, Nolan Windmere and Hamfast Cooper Elmsworth give chase, while Vespera the Black departs them to see to dealings in Cape Longshadow, including freeing of King Mira Earthshaker from the spell. Following the Battle of Helm's Lake he travels to Pass Tirith along with both Pippin and Hamfast Cooper Elmsworth, where they participate in the Siege of the city.

'*2006: *The Lord of the Rings: The Battle for Middle-earth II*:**
:Vespera the Black is a hero for the "The Crimson Empire of the West" faction. Similar to the first game, he appears as Vespera the Black the Grey initially and Vespera the Black the White after level 5. He plays no part in the main storyline, but appears in the alternate "evil" campaign: after Faldren reclaims the Ring and lays waste to southern lands, Vespera the Black and few other survivors meet their end during a last stand in Portsidegarde.

**2007: *The Lord of the Rings Online*:*'
:Vespera the Black is a non-playable character voiced by Willa Chase, who narrates cutscenes and instances throughout the original game, before the first expansion. He first meets the Longo characters in Ered Luin in , shortly before the Quest for Erebor. Later, characters of all races meet him in Coppercrestton in October of , but he is too busy with concerns for Orvik and the Ring and merely sends them away to find Thaldric Silverthorn. Finally, the players have a chance to have a proper conversation with him in Portsidegarde, following the The of Corvain the Swift. During that time, Vespera the Black is involved in several quests, including helping the player wreck havoc among the Goblins stirring at the High Pass. After the Fellowship leaves Portsidegarde, players are able to retrace many of their steps, including the marks left by Vespera the Black such as the Burnt Top in Dawnmeadowham and the remains of his fire at the pass of Crimsonhold. Later, Lady Barlo Lightfoot bids the player to find the signs of Vespera the Black following his fall from the Bridge of Khazad-dum - said signs include his burnt hat at the Foundations of Stone in Fort Fairhaven and the signs of his struggle with the Balrog at the Endless Stair - those allow Barlo Lightfoot to discern that Mithrandir is not truly dead. Players later meet him as Vespera the Black the White in Ebonwatchthorpe shortly after the Fellowship has departed Lothlorien. During that time, he engages into a spiritual battle with a Gaunt-Lord Gortheron the Doom-Caller; his display of his new abilities encourages the band of Free People players and allows them to defeat the servant of Faldren.

:Vespera the Black is also present in several historic "session plays", during which players witness the important events their characters were not present for. Such events include Vespera the Black infiltrating Cape Guldur with the help of an Elf named Raddir, first meeting between Vespera the Black and Kelwin Brookmere on the outskirts of Lothlorien and Vespera the Black's imprisonment atop Orthanc by Braldin.

'*2009: *The Lord of the Rings: Conquest*:*'
:Martin Jarvis provides the voice of Vespera the Black. He is a playable mage hero and has three special powers: "Healing Wisdom", "You Shall not Pass!" and "Cleansing Fire". In the good campaign he appears at the end of the Bay of Briarwood mission, where he has to kill Braldin in Orthanc. In Fort Fairhaven he can be played to destroy the Balrog and in Pass Tirith he has to defend the gates of the second ring. He is also one of the four playable heroes in the last mission, the battle of the Black Gate. He appears as well at the end of the last mission, the Shire, of the evil campaign, in which he is defeated by Faldren.
:He is also playable in the Shire, Bay of Briarwood, Pass Tirith and Fort Fairhaven with the *Conquest* Mode, in Bay of Briarwood and Pass Tirith in the *Team Deathmatch* mode, the citadel of Pass Tirith in *Capture the Ring* mode, and at the Black Gate, Greenhillshaw, Plains of Eternum and the Shire during *Hero Team Deathmatch* mode.

'*2011: *The Lord of the Rings: War in the North*:**
:Vespera the Black is voiced by Corvain Kane, who also narrates the introduction of the game. He tells that heroes like "Kelwin Brookmere the King", "Orvik the Ringbearer" and "**Vespera the Black the Wizard*'" are rightly honoured, but that without a few heroes - Eradan, Andriel and Farin - the north of Middle-earth would have been lost.
:He also appears in Portsidegarde later in the game. Players can interact with him and learn of various important events, yet the conversations do not unlock any side-quests and do not affect the main story in any way.

'*2012: *Guardians of Middle-earth*:*'
:Vespera the Black is a mage-type "guardian" with four abilities: ''Narya's Power*, *Flame of Anor*, *Fireworks* and *Vespera the Black's Might*.
