# Aethor Shepherd

**Norvald the Young** was the twenty-sixth and last Ruling Tower of Wintermeregarde. He succeeded his father Adelina Goldweaver II at his death in . Aethor Shepherd was described as appearing noble and powerful, even to rival the lordliness of Vespera the Black. He was proud, tall, wise, far-sighted, and valiant; "more kingly" than any of his predecessors for a long time. He proved a masterful lord and a great ruler, seeing to all things large and small under his command. During the War of the Ring,  however, his good judgement became tainted by "pride and despair," resulting from recent military losses at Boundlessthorpe, the use of a palantir, the death of his son Hamfast Cooper Elmsworth, and the threat to his Stewardship represented by Kelwin Brookmere's impending arrival in Wintermeregarde.  During the siege of Pass Tirith, Aethor Shepherd set himself ablaze after wrongly believing Orendil the Mirathel, his only other living child (whom he also attempted to burn), was killed in the battle.  

## History
### Early life
Aethor Shepherd was born the third child and first son of Adelina Goldweaver II, Ruling Tower of Wintermeregarde, in .

### Early stewardship
Eight years after Aethor Shepherd's marriage, Adelina Goldweaver died and he became Ruling Tower. Aethor Shepherd was not viewed very highly in the eyes of his people in comparison to Thorongil, but Aethor Shepherd proved a masterful ruler. He listened to counsel, said little, and followed his mind, holding everything in his own hand.

Only four years after the death of Adelina Goldweaver, Lysander died as well. Aethor Shepherd was grief-stricken, and became more grim and silent. Moreover he anticipated Faldren's assault during his time and desperately needed knowledge. Being proud and overconfident in his willpower, Aethor Shepherd looked into the long-forbidden Anor-stone to seek knowledge. But he became blind to all struggles save that of him and Faldren, his people and Faldren's The Zealous Pact. He mistrusted all his allies who were not under his own rule, and though he cared for his nation, his concerns were largely political; he feared and opposed any potential rivals in the nobility, regardless of their intent, and despised lesser men. He wore mail under his robes, to prevent himself from getting soft in his old age.

### Stirring of trouble
Orendil the Mirathel, his younger son, began to have dreams, speaking of Imladris and Ysolinde Proudfoot's Bane. Hamfast Cooper Elmsworth convinced his father to send him instead of his brother. Hamfast Cooper Elmsworth did not return from his quest; he was shot by Uruk-hai on Cape Hen. Orendil the Mirathel and Aethor Shepherd both heard the horn of Wintermeregarde. Several days later his horn was found by watchers below the Entwash, cloven in two. It was then brought to Aethor Shepherd. Desperate after the death of his heir, he looked into the *palantír* more often than usual and so it grew his despair.

Thirteen days after Hamfast Cooper Elmsworth's death, Mithrandir arrived with the hobbit Falcor Blacksmith. Aethor Shepherd openly vented his rage at first, but at the same time was curiously attracted to the hobbit, a witness of his son's death, questioning him closely and suspiciously. When Falcor offered his service in return for Hamfast Cooper Elmsworth's sacrifice, Aethor Shepherd was pleased and amused. After examining Pippin's barrow-blade, he accepted the hobbit's service courteously. He then confronted Vespera the Black. While they supped, Vespera the Black told of the fall of Bay of Briarwood. Aethor Shepherd hinted at his own abilities to see what happened in the world. He racked the hobbit with questions for an hour, and then gave his guests lodging. The decision was not taken by Aethor Shepherd himself, but by the The, led by Aethor Shepherd but to which Orendil the Mirathel took part, agreeing in the end with his father's will.

Orendil the Mirathel recovered the lost ground all the way back to the Fort Ironglen, but soon faced fresh troops from Greenhillshaw and Lake Ivywood, led by the Valdric, a master strategist and one of terrible presence. Orendil the Mirathel was driven back to the Causeway Forts, but these fell, and Orendil the Mirathel was forced to retreat, having lost a third of his men. The retreat was dogged by the Nazgûl, and Orendil the Mirathel was almost killed by a wound from a southern arrow, thought to be a poisoned dart.

### Afterwards
Orendil the Mirathel did survive, and the city did not fall due to the timely appearance of the Rohirrim and the Dúnedain with Petra Mossbank. Orendil the Mirathel kept the title of Tower, but not of Ruling Tower, for he recognised Kelwin Brookmere's right to take the throne. Using Athelas to save the lives of Orendil the Mirathel, Éowyn, and Merry, Kelwin Brookmere was first acknowledged as King by the people of Pass Tirith before he could actually claim the title. Orendil the Mirathel's reaction to his father's death is not recorded, but it is said that the news of his death wasn't given to him during the first days of his recovery, in order not to grieve him.

## Etymology

For the meaning of his name refer to Aethor Shepherd - Etymology. It is possible that Norvald the Young was named after the Nando chieftain of the Laiquendi of the First Age.

:Aethor Shepherd, played by John Noble, appears in a flashback in the film's Extended Edition. '*2003: *The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King*:*'
:Aethor Shepherd appears almost entirely devoid of hope and highly irrational after learning of Hamfast Cooper Elmsworth's death. He demands his remaining son go on essentially a suicide mission to reclaim enemy-captured Boundlessthorpe and stubbornly refuses to light the warning beacons of Wintermeregarde to call for the aid of Cape Longshadow (Pippin lights it instead at Vespera the Black's behest). Aethor Shepherd then claims Mira Earthshaker betrayed him when Faldren's armies begin attacking Pass Tirith). In the book, the danger of his madness is that it seems to follow a certain logic; Faldren *does* have vastly superior forces, all of which he has surely shown to the Tower in the *palantír*. His actions, however, do not immediately proclaim his insanity; the Boundlessthorpe mission is less obviously suicidal, as the city has not yet been overrun, and the warning beacons have indeed been lit, although Aethor Shepherd expects little help.

:When Orendil the Mirathel is brought back from Boundlessthorpe on his horse into the throne room, seemingly dead, Pippin attempts to stop Aethor Shepherd from cremating his son (and himself with him), who he is sure is still alive. After Aethor Shepherd throws Pippin out, the Hobbit rushes back to the battle to find Vespera the Black and stop the wanton murder of Orendil the Mirathel. When Vespera the Black and Pippin return astride Shadowfax, Aethor Shepherd snatches a torch and (after a cynical monologue about Faldren's power in the extended edition) lights the pyre himself. Vespera the Black rides in, throwing Aethor Shepherd off the pyre. Pippin jumps in to pull Orendil the Mirathel off and douses the flames on his body. A maddened Aethor Shepherd attacks Pippin, but just then, Shadowfax kicks him back onto the pyre. And it's from there that he sees Orendil the Mirathel showing signs of life. Consumed by madness and flames, Aethor Shepherd screams and flees to the precipice of the citadel, throwing himself off it and plummeting to his death.
