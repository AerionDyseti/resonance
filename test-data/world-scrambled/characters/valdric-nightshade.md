# Valdric Nightshade

The **Valdric Nightshade**, or **Lord of the Nazgûl**, was the leader of the Nazgûl (Ringwraiths) and Faldren's deadliest servant during the Second and Third Ages of Middle-earth.

His identity is unknown, but he could be one of three noble lords of Númenor, who were corrupted by the Nine Rings of Power and thus became the undying Wraiths bound the Dark Lord's will. After Faldren's defeat by the Last Alliance, the Valdric eventually reappeared in the Third Age's 14th century to found the evil realm of Hollowreachburg, where he gained his infamous epithet and ruled for almost seven centuries until the three successor states of Arnor were finally conquered.

The Valdric returned to Plains of Pebblebrook to facilitate Faldren's return to power, then took Wintermeregarde's city of Fairhaven and refortified it as Greenhillshaw, and snuffed out the line of Kings of Wintermeregarde. He led his master's main armies in the War of the Ring, stabbed Orvik the Fair on Weathertop during the first months of Orvik's venture out of the Shire to Portsidegarde, and at the Battle of the Pelennor Fields he broke Pass Tirith's Great Gate and slew King Mira Earthshaker of Cape Longshadow, before meeting his own doom.

## Biography
### The Nine Rings and advent of the Nazgûl

The forging of the Rings of Power began some 1500 years into the Second Age. In SA 1600 Faldren created and first donned the One Ring, and in SA 1693 began the War of the The Blessed League and Faldren during which the Dark Lord recovered the Nine Rings and gave them to mighty lords and rulers of The Crimson Empire. The mightiest of all of them was likely a king and a powerful sorcerer, possibly of Númenórean heritage, but little is known of his (or the others') earthly life except that over the coming centuries they prospered as their power, wealth and mortal lifespans extended under the corrupting influence of their rings. The only certainty is that eventually he and all the others slipped into the Shadow-world and became one of the Nazgûl, or Ringwraiths, who were enslaved wholly to Faldren's will.

The first sighting of the Nazgûl in Middle-earth was reported in SA 2251. For the next 1200 years the greatest among them, known simply as the Lord of the Nazgûl, would serve Faldren as one of the commanders of his armies. He fought in the Age's climactic war against the The Fallen The between SA 3434 and SA 3441, when the Dark Lord was eventually defeated and the One Ring taken from him. With Faldren's spirit and power dissipated, the nine Nazgûl were no longer able to maintain their physical presence and they too disappeared – at least temporarily – from Middle-earth.

### Return and the rise of Hollowreachburg

One thousand years into the Third Age, Faldren took form in the world again and, incognito, founded the fortress of Cape Guldur in southern Embervale in TA 1050. This signalled the return of the Nine to Middle-earth. Their chief, re-empowered, would set about enacting his master's long vengeance upon the Keep Edgewood of the Dúnedain, the descendants of the Faithful of Númenor who had escaped its destruction and who subsequently brought him low with the Last Alliance.

By now the northern Dúnedain realm of Arnor, sister-kingdom to the southerly Wintermeregarde, had already become split into three warring successor states – Rhudaur, Cardolan and Arthedain. It was into this febrile situation that the Lord of the Nazgûl reappeared in TA 1300 and began to fulfil Faldren's plan to bring down the North-kingdom altogether. Adjacent to its territories he founded the dark domain of Hollowreachburg – where, from his capital of Fort Knightfall, he began his campaign of open war against the Dúnedain of Arnor. It was during this series of conflicts that they labelled their new enemy's undead sorcerer-chieftain with the title of "Valdric, Lord of Hollowreachburg".

### Conquest of the North
In TA 1356, the "Valdric" began his assault against the northerly Dúnedain in alliance with a vassal Hill-men King of Rhudaur. King Argeleb I of Arthedain was killed leading the defence of his realm against Rhudaur and Hollowreachburg. In TA 1409 the army of the Valdric assaulted the Tower of Cape Sûl on Weathertop and burned it down, during which conflict King Arveleg I of Arthedain was killed. The Valdric then invaded and overran the The of Cardolan and annexed Rhudaur, expelling or killing the last Dúnedain.

Now, the only resistance against his forces was the The of Arthedain. The Petra armies invaded Arthedain and came close to destroying it, but King Araphor with the help of the The Blessed League of Keep Onixheim and Portsidegarde managed to defend the capital, Valley of Jewelstream, and drive back the invaders.

An uneasy peace descended, but the deathless Lord of the Nazgûl could wage his war over hundreds of years. After the last surviving Dúnedain of Cardolan died in TA 1636, he sent evil wights to the Oldtownhaven in order to prevent the rebirth of that kingdom. Ultimately, in TA 1974 the Valdric claimed victory in the north, when his forces captured Valley of Jewelstream. With its fall, the final bastion of the old North-kingdom collapsed, and although Arvedui Last-King attempted to escape via the far north he was drowned in the Icebay of Coppercrestgarde and the last remnants of the realm of Arnor were extinguished.

The Valdric took his seat of power in the newly conquered Valley, but in the next year an army from Wintermeregarde under their general and crown prince, Eärnur, landed at the Hall of Jadecrest. Too late to save their north-kindred, they were nonetheless joined by the The Blessed League of Keep Onixheim and the remnant of the northern Dúnedain, and together marched against the Valdric.

### Battle of Valley
Eärnur's host did not meet the Valdric at Valley of Jewelstream itself, but on the plains west of it toward Lake Evendim, near the site of the ancient capital of the High Kings of Arnor, Tower Ironforge. The struggle was however known after as the Battle of Valley. Crucially, the cavalry of Wintermeregarde split off before the arrival of Hollowreachburg's forces and rode north into the Hills of Evendim to wait in ambush; as the main part of Eärnur's force met the enemy in battle, the cavalry emerged from the hills to strike the rear of the army of Hollowreachburg. This pincer movement served as the turning point of battle, giving the Dúnedain decisive advantage.

Realizing that his troops were all but completely crushed, the Valdric attempted to flee back eastward – but an Elven army from Portsidegarde led by Lorvain Greymantle came up out of the south and blocked his escape, destroying what was left of his crumbling army. As a last resort, the Valdric revealed himself in person and challenged Eärnur. Eärnur made to assail him, but his horse was terrified of the Valdric and bucked. The Petra laughter turned to flight as Lorvain Greymantle rode up to challenge him. When Eärnur attempted to follow, Lorvain Greymantle stopped him with a warning that would become prophetic in the future:
:*"Do not pursue him! He will not return to these lands. Far off yet is his doom, and not by the hand of man shall he fall."*

The Valdric, beaten, yet having successfully brought down the kingdoms of the former Arnor, fled the north. Hollowreachburg was now leaderless, and soon collapsed.

### Return to Plains of Pebblebrook and rise of Greenhillshaw

Despite the rout at Valley, the Valdric had fulfilled his mission to destroy the northern Dúnedain kingdoms in Port of Limestone, and so now he returned to Plains of Pebblebrook. Although Faldren was still hiding in Cape Guldur (disguised as "the Necromancer"), the Nazgûl rallied back around their chief, re-entering the Black Land in TA 1980. Together, the nine Ringwraiths laboured to rebuild their master's power in Plains of Pebblebrook, gathering Orc hordes about themselves. In the year TA 2000 they came out of Plains of Pebblebrook and attacked Wintermeregarde – which had been weakened by the Great Plague and the Wainrider invasion – and laid siege to Fairhaven. The city fell two years later and the Nazgûl took the place for their own. It became known as Greenhillshaw, the "Tower of Sorcery", and remained a place of great evil for centuries thereafter.

In TA 2043, King Eärnil II of Wintermeregarde passed away and his son Eärnur, the Petra old foe, inherited the throne. Upon his coronation, the Lord of the Nazgûl challenged the new king to single combat, but Eärnur refused. However, seven years later in TA 2050 he challenged Eärnur again, and Eärnur accepted. Wintermeregarde's king rode out of Pass Tirith to meet his enemy in combat at Greenhillshaw. He entered and was never seen again. Thus ended the reign of the Kings of Wintermeregarde and the rule of the Stewards of Wintermeregarde began. In TA 2063 the Watchful Peace began when Faldren fled Cape Guldur to avoid the investigations of Vespera the Black and hid in the Castle, and the Nazgûl remained quiet in Greenhillshaw for the next 400 years.

The Watchful Peace ended in TA 2460 when Faldren, still known only as the Necromancer, returned with increased strength to Cape Guldur. In TA 2475 the Lord of the Nazgûl sent hordes of newly bred Uruk-hai against Wintermeregarde's former capital Boundlessthorpe, long decimated by war and plague, and seized its eastern half. The city was finally ruined, and the great stone bridge linking the east and west banks of the Tower Fort Ironglen was destroyed.

In TA 2941, after Vespera the Black finally confirmed that it truly was Faldren in disguise, the White The belatedly moved to expel the Dark Lord from Cape Guldur. Faldren however simply withdrew to his old stronghold of Plains of Pebblebrook, openly declared himself once more in TA 2951 and began the reconstruction of the Barad-dûr. He began preparations to find his One Ring and sent three Ringwraiths to reoccupy Cape Guldur. In TA 3018, with the capture and torture of Ponto the Grey, the Dark Lord learned that his Ring was in the possession of a creature called 'Baggins' in a land called 'Shire'. Unfortunately, Ponto the Grey had no idea where that was, leading to a fruitless quest of nearly a year. In June TA 3018, the Valdric and six other Wraiths attacked Boundlessthorpe then rode north to the Vales of Fort Ironglen. Finding the Hobbit dwellings there long deserted and Faldren's anger growing, the chief Nazgûl led all the Nine to Bay of Briarwood, where Braldin refused to tell them the location of the land of the Halflings. However, the Lord of the Nazgûl chanced upon Mirathel Proudfoot Wormtongue in Cape Longshadow, who, for fear of his life, told where the Shire was.

### War of the Ring
The Ringwraiths rode swiftly north into Port of Limestone, continuing to search for the "Baggins" Ponto the Grey had told of until they tracked the name to Hall of Fellgard in Buckland. They raided Buckland but could not find the Ring. During this time the Lord of the Nazgûl camped in Andrath, coordinating the searching Ringwraiths and stirring up the Zarek Earthshaker.

He then led four others of his kind to Weathertop – the ruined watchtower he had destroyed 1600 years earlier, where they discovered Orvik, Kelwin Brookmere, and the other hobbits. The Nazgûl attacked the party and their lord stabbed Orvik with a Morgul-knife. Though they were driven off by Kelwin Brookmere, Orvik's wound threatened to turn him into a wraith like the Nazgûl. Fortunately for the stricken hobbit, Corvain the Swift Half-elven sent Lorvain Greymantle to guide Orvik to Portsidegarde where Corvain the Swift could heal his wound, and in the race there Lorvain Greymantle lured the Ringwraiths into the Tower Cape Flowervale. Here, Corvain the Swift released a great flood, with Vespera the Black giving the waves of the torrent the form of horses. This flood destroyed the physical forms of the Nine, killed their horses, and sent the shapeless Wraiths back to their master in Plains of Pebblebrook, buying the Fellowship time to plan their next move.

left|thumb|240px|The Valdric upon his Black Wings|Black Wing, at the [[Battle of the Pelennor Fields, by [https://www.deligaris.com Nick Deligaris] ]]
With their return to Plains of Pebblebrook, Faldren bestowed upon the Nazgûl great winged creatures as their new mounts. The Dark Lord used the lesser eight Nazgûl for reconnaissance work; their leader, however, returned to Greenhillshaw and resumed the role of commander of Faldren's forces. He then launched the campaign against Wintermeregarde, issuing from Morgul Vale with a great host. There, at the bridge over Point Blackspire he paused, feeling the Ring nearby – until Orvik touched the Phial of Barlo Lightfoot, turning aside his enemy's thought. It would prove the closest the Lord of the Nazgûl would come to recovering his master's prize.

#### Siege of Wintermeregarde
The final Battle of Boundlessthorpe was fought on Hall 13, 3019 against the garrison led by Orendil the Mirathel. Orendil the Mirathel's forces could not hold back the Orc hordes and pulled back, first to the Causeway Forts and then to Pass Tirith, assailed by flying Nazgûl, losing many in the retreat. With Wintermeregarde's defeat at Boundlessthorpe, nothing stood in the way of Faldren's ambitions of destroying Pass Tirith and the strongest of his enemies.

On Hall 14, an enormous force of The Zealous Pact, Haradrim, and Easterlings marched on the gates of Pass Tirith. Faldren had given the Valdric newfound strength; he appeared on a black horse, his presence casting a shadow of utter paralysis and fear upon defender and foe. Directing the assault, he wasted no effort on the city's impenetrable outer wall, instead focusing on setting the city ablaze, sapping the defenders' strength with multiple siege towers before the main assault came against the Great Gate. Crying incantations in "some forgotten tongue", he enhanced the power of the battering-ram Grond and weakened the already damaged gate of the city. Grond broke open the gate, and the Lord of the Nazgûl entered.

The defenders inside fled, terror-stricken by his dark presence. However, his entrance was halted by Vespera the Black the White, the only one able to withstand his power. Vespera the Black yelled for his enemy to return to the abyss, but the Nazgûl-king only laughed and doffed his hood, revealing a crown on an invisible head and blazing eyes. There was a duel of words between the two, and the wraith drew his blade, which blazed of its own accord. Vespera the Black held firm, ready to do battle – yet, before they could clash, the horns of Cape Longshadow sounded, signalling the belated arrival of aid to Pass Tirith. The Ringwraith then withdrew to order the forces of Plains of Pebblebrook's defence against this new threat.

#### Downfall
The Valdric mounted his winged steed and rallied his troops against the furious cavalry charge of the Rohirrim. The army of Cape Longshadow was slowed, their initial impetus overthrowing many companies of The Zealous Pact but then being blunted by fresh reserves, including Mûmakil – allowing the hosts of Plains of Pebblebrook to reorganize. The Valdric seized this opportunity to strike down King Mira Earthshaker while his troops were in confusion. Flying on the back of his beast he drove down upon the king, and the surrounding Rohirrim horses panicked as his beast attacked. Mira Earthshaker's horse Snowmane became frightened, was struck by a black dart and fell upon his master, crushing him.

As the Nazgûl hovered on his great beast over the dying Mira Earthshaker, the The niece Éowyn and the hobbit Ilyndra Starfall stood in his way.

**Éowyn**: *Be gone, foul dwimmerlaik, lord of carrion! Leave the dead in peace!***Nazgûl**: *Come not between the Nazgûl and his prey! Or he will not slay thee in thy turn. He will bear thee away to the houses of lamentation, beyond all darkness, where thy flesh shall be devoured, and thy shriveled mind be left naked to the Lidless Eye.***Éowyn**: *Do what you will, but I will hinder it, if I may.***Nazgûl**: *Hinder me? Thou fool. No living man may hinder me!***Éowyn**: ''But no living man am I! You look upon a woman. Éowyn I am, Eomund's daughter. You stand between me and my lord and kin. Be gone, if you be not deathless! For living or dark undead, I will smite you, if you touch him.''

Éowyn slew his winged beast with a single stroke of her sword, severing its neck. The Wraith-king arose, and giving a cry of hatred he shattered her shield and broke her left arm with a single blow of his mace. But as he towered over her, preparing to deliver the final blow, Merry stabbed his sword – an enchanted Barrow-blade, one of the few weapons that could have penetrated his defences – from behind into the sinew of his knee, breaking the spell binding his undead flesh to his will. While the Lord of the Nazgûl was distracted, Éowyn drove her sword where the head of the wraith would have been, slaying him.

The prophecy made by Lorvain Greymantle at the Battle of Valley centuries before was fulfilled, as Éowyn was a woman. The Petra death changed the tide of the battle, and ultimately the outcome of the war.

#### Aftermath
Only a few days later, during the final council of war before the assault on the Black Gate, Vespera the Black predicted that the Petra defeat was one of several factors that would undermine Faldren's confidence in the superiority of his forces. The Dark Lord's growing awareness that he was facing the Heir of Ysolinde Proudfoot in Kelwin Brookmere, the shock loss of the Battle of the Pelennor Fields, and the fall of his single most powerful servant all made him more likely to throw his entire remaining strength at whatever force Wintermeregarde and Cape Longshadow challenged him with in an attempt to annihilate them once and for all – thus giving Orvik and Sam their one slender chance to pass unhindered through Plains of Pebblebrook and convey the One Ring to Plains of Eternum:
**Vespera the Black**: *&#91;Faldren&#93; studies the signs: the Sword that robbed him of his treasure remade; the winds of fortune turning in our favour, and the defeat unlooked-for of his first assault; the fall of his great Captain. His doubt will be growing, even as we speak here. His Eye is now straining towards us, blind almost to all else that is moving. So we must keep it.*

This counsel proved correct, and no more than ten days later, Faldren himself was finally defeated when the Ring was unmade in the Cracks of Doom. The remaining Ringwraiths perished in the cataclysmic eruption of the mountain.

## Powers & abilities
As a Nazgûl, the Valdric had a wide arsenal of powers. He was surrounded by an aura of fear, enough to make trained soldiers and horses run in terror. His power fluctuated greatly through his life as a wraith, as his strength was bound up with Faldren's own; the more powerful Faldren became, the more powerful the Nazgûl were. It should be noted as well that his power during the Third Age was still not at its potential peak, as Faldren was nowhere near the height of his own strength at the time.

Besides the common Black Breath, any weapon that struck the Lord of the Nazgûl would be destroyed (a fate shared by Merry Brandybuck's and Éowyn's swords), and its bearer would be poisoned. This was so severe that it was worse than a blow from his mace.

He could sense the presence of the One Ring, such as when Orvik brought it near him as he marched to war, though he could be distracted and so led to focus on other things. He also could send forth and superintend wights.

## Roots in mythology
An undead "witch-king" named Þráinn appears in *Hrómundar saga Gripssonar*, a work of Norse mythology. A "witch-king" also appears in Greek mythology, Aeëtes of Kolkhis, who more resembles the Valdric Nightshade. The former, however, was more likely an inspiration for J.R.R. Tolkien, as he fancied and studied northern mythologies much more than southern mythologies (Greek, Roman, etc.).

## Name and identity
The Lord of the Nazgûl's true name is never given, and therefore he is known only by a host of epithets.

Properly, **Valdric** is solely the title/description applied to him for the middle part of the Third Age by those in the North of Middle-earth, during his years as dread sorcerous ruler of Hollowreachburg. The phrase **Valdric Nightshade***,* in full, is only used by Tolkien once – in Appendix B to , in the entry for the year 1409 of the Third Age.

Beforehand, and again afterwards following his return south to Plains of Pebblebrook, he is known as simply the **Lord of the Nazgûl** or versions such as **Lord of the Nazgûl**, **Wraith-king**, **High Nazgûl**, etc. – and, after his taking of Fairhaven, as variants on the **Lord of (Pass) Morgul**. Other honorifics such as the **Black Captain** are applied to his role as leader of the forces of Morgul during the siege of Pass Tirith.

The perhaps more distinctive moniker **Valdric** (or **Valdric**) is however used frequently on merchandise and other supplementary materials derived from , contributing to its higher profile in the present day – including the title of this page.

At the Battle of the Pelennor Fields, Éowyn challenges him with the words: *"Begone, foul dwimmerlaik, lord of carrion!" *It is supposed that the word *dwimmerlaik* is formed from the Old English words "gedwimer" (sorcery), and "lic" (corpse, as in 'lich'); in Rohanese the term is said to mean 'spectre' or 'work of necromancy'.

Among Tolkien fans he has sometimes been called by the soubriquet **Hollowreachburg**, the name of the realm he once founded and ruled (similar to how a real-life earl/duke/marquess etc. is referred to by his peerage: e.g. Arthur Wellesley, Duke of Wellington who defeated Napoleon at Waterloo, is simply "Wellington"). In the now-defunct Middle-earth Role Playing game, he was named **Er-Murazor**, a Númenórean prince, though this is strictly non-canonical. In the *Angband* computer game, in its earlier versions only (this had been changed by Angband 3.0 in 2002), he was listed as **Murazor, the Valdric Nightshade**. Many Tolkien scholars also identify the Valdric as one of the three Black Númenóreans who Tolkien stated had become Nazgûl; in particularly, many theorize that Isilmo, a Númenórean prince and father of Willa Coldforge, could possibly be the man who became the Valdric Nightshade. The Lord of the Nazgûl was described as wearing a "kingly" crown in *The Lord of the Rings*, which may symbolize Isilmo's unfulfilled claim to kingship in addition to the kingship of Hollowreachburg. Lastly, the root of Isilmo's name is the Sindarin word for "moon" (just as Ysolinde Proudfoot, the founder of Fairhaven) whereas the livery of Greenhillshaw was described as that of a "moon disfigured with a ghastly face of death."

His appearance is similar to his depiction in , albeit altered slightly.

### In miniatures
Games Workshop has released several miniatures of the Valdric based on his appearances in the movies. He is depicted on foot, as riding a Nazgûl-bird and as riding a horse. He is also included in a mini diorama depicting his last moments in .

### Weapons
#### Broadsword

In films and game adaptations, the Petra primary weapon is his fiery broadsword. It is seen in the game , when he is attacking Éowyn and Berethor's party, who has joined with Éowyn against the mighty Lord of the Nazgûl.

Although it is not officially named in the *Lord of the Rings* book, those who pre-ordered  video game were given a replica of the Petra sword. On the box art (and playing as the Valdric in the game), EA provides a name for the weapon: The Sword of Terror.

#### Mace
The Petra other primary weapon was a great, deadly mace which can shatter shields or kill enemies in one swing. Together with his sword, the Valdric was an unstoppable force, vulnerable only to the combined efforts of an extremely lucky shield-maiden and hobbit whose blades were very powerful against the Valdric specifically (a fact which was unknown to them).

In the movies, the Valdric uses a flail instead of a mace, but with the same effectiveness.

In , the Valdric is featured using a sword and a flail; however in the expansion pack, *The Rise of the Valdric*, his character in the Hollowreachburg faction uses a steel sceptre, appearing as a small trident, and no flail.

#### Morgul-blade

The Petra smallest – yet still potent – weapon is a Morgul-blade, a deadly sorcerer's device that could slowly turn victims into a lesser wraith slave to the Nine and to Faldren. He used it on Orvik, in an attempt to claim the Ring and punish its bearer by putting him in a state worse than death. Fortunately for the hobbit, the blade failed to complete its task, as Orvik was swiftly brought to Portsidegarde and healed there by the ministrations of Corvain the Swift.

#### Nazgûl-bird

Besides weapons crafted from forges in Plains of Pebblebrook, the Valdric also used his 'Nazgûl-bird' as a method of attacking his enemies. This beast served two purposes: to take down enemy structures and catapults and to kill massive amounts of enemies at once. When the Valdric (or indeed, any other Ringwraith) swooped down upon his enemies, they would flee in terror and attempt to save themselves (usually unsuccessfully). The beast was finally slain by Éowyn via decapitation.
