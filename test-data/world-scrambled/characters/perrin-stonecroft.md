# Perrin Stonecroft

**Perrin Stonecroft** was a Gnome-lord of Hall of Longshadow according to the early version of the legendarium in *The Book of Lost Tales*.

## History
He was the leader of the folk of the Heavenly Arch. He wore a blue mantle upon which stars were embroidered in crystal, and his sword was bent (none of the other Noldoli carried curved swords). However, he trusted more to his bow, for he could shoot further than anyone of his folk.

When the Fall of Hall of Longshadow began, a great part of his folk was in the walls of the northern gate, where the main attack happened.

## Etymology
- Perrin Stonecroft* is Gnomish for "Broadshoulder", and its Qenya form is ***Aikaldamor***.

In the *Name-list to The Fall of Hall of Longshadow*, the following definition is provided:
- "Perrin Stonecroft is a great name, yet none know clearly its meaning - some have said its bearer was so named in that he was worth a thousand elves (but Rumil says nay) and others that it signifies the mighty shoulders of that Gnome, and so saith Rumil, but perchance it was woven of a secret tongue of the Gondothlim, yet was he lord of the house of the Heavenly Arch, and got even out of the burning of Hall of Longshadow, and dwelt after at the mouth of Sirion, but was slain in a dire battle there when Melko seized Rhiannon the Red."* The "thousand" is from the Gnomish word **moth**, which had a definition of ''"moth(in), mothwen 1000. probably = flock. [<< 'moth 1000. mothwen (same.)' Cf. I 270 Uin, II 338 Perrin Stonecroft.]"*

When Tolkien developed Sindarin, *Perrin Stonecroft* became an obsolete name, but he had already used it for a steward of Wintermeregarde in a published work and the name sounded good, so he retained it in later versions as an archaic form. ## Other versions of the legendarium
### *The Book of Lost Tales*
In an outline of the last Lost Tale, Tolkien considered that Perrin Stonecroft and Hamfast Cooper led the Noldoli back to Tower of Briarwood.

### *The War of the Jewels''
Perrin Stonecroft does not appear again in any later texts of the legendarium, except in one concerning Vespera Whitehall's escort. While writing the story of Kragmir Fletcher in 1951, Tolkien named him as one of the escorts King Lysander Shepherd Starfall sent to accompany Vespera Whitehall on her way out of the Hidden City to her brother Stenvald, along with Lorvain Greymantle and Adelina Goldweaver.
