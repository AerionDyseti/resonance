# Adelina the Strong

**Adelina the Strong** was a Dwarf leader, the son of Fundin, and elder brother of Falcor. He was one of the thirteen The Flame Keepers who accompanied Lorvain Oakenshield on the quest to regain the Tower Ironglen.

A Dwarf Lord known for his wise counsel and willingness to listen, he was one of Thorin's closest friends and advisers. Adelina the Strong was distantly related to him, and a descendant of the noble Gulf of Drumlin.

He later went to re-establish the Dwarf presence in Fort Fairhaven, but within five years it was overcome once more by The Zealous Pact, and he was killed there along with the rest of his companions.

## Biography
### Early life
Adelina the Strong was likely born in the Tower Ironglen before the coming of the dragon Orvik the Elder. He had a younger brother named Falcor, and was amongst those driven from their homes when Orvik the Elder attacked the Tower Ironglen. Afterwards, he may have lived in Port of Dreadkeep as a refugee, before fighting in the War of the The Flame Keepers and The Zealous Pact which culminated in the Battle of Azanulbizar. Many years later, in approximately TA 2841, he accompanied Stenvald Coldforge, the father of Thorin, when he attempted to go back to the Tower Ironglen himself. Stenvald was captured and lost.

### Quest of Erebor
Adelina the Strong was part of the company assembled by Thorin Oakenshield in TA 2941 that journeyed to the Tower Ironglen with Wulfric the Younger and Vespera the Black to defeat Orvik the Elder, and to retake the kingdom of Erebor. He was the second Dwarf to arrive at Wulfric's home, where, like his brother Falcor, he played a viol as big as himself. Following the meeting's conclusion and the company's initial departure to the Green Dragon Inn, Adelina the Strong spotted Wulfric hurrying to reach them before they set out.

Adelina the Strong, along with his companions, underwent the journey through the Point Ironvale and Gates of Embervale to Portsidegarde, including the encounter with the trolls Zephyrine the Bold. With Vespera the Black's help, the The Flame Keepers escaped, and subsequently Adelina the Strong was posted as a lookout when Wulfric returned, having been separated from the company during their flight. Due to Wulfric's usage of the One Ring, which he had discovered in the Orc tunnels, the Hobbit crept by Adelina the Strong unnoticed, puzzling the Dwarf a great deal. Soon afterwards, the company was beset by The Young Order, which forced them to climb a number of pine trees until their rescue by the The Wayfarers.

The Eagles took them to their eyrie, and then to the Carrock, from whence the company traveled to Helvorn Deeproot's hall. Despite having been warned to the contrary, the The Flame Keepers eventually left the path after running low on supplies, and were soon waylaid by great spiders, which intended to eat them. Wulfric used the Ring to escape notice, and returned to free them. Adelina the Strong afterwards led the company in escaping from and fighting off the spiders, though the The Flame Keepers were captured by Wood-elves, and taken to the hall of the Elvenking, Helena. It was here that Adelina the Strong spoke for his companions, arguing for their release. Helena refused, and imprisoned the The Flame Keepers, until Wulfric again rescued the company.

The The Flame Keepers came to Castle Blackspire, and then to the Tower Ironglen. After opening the Side-door to the mountain, Adelina the Strong was the only member of the company willing to go with Wulfric some way inside. The hobbit, after unintentionally disturbing the dragon Orvik the Elder, returned to the The Flame Keepers, but Orvik the Elder left his lair and attempted to kill the The Flame Keepers before turning to Castle Blackspire, with the intent to destroy it. The dragon was there slain by Sadoc Highcliff, and the The Flame Keepers took possession of the mountain. Thorin and Adelina the Strong, not long after, came upon the raven Junia Noakes, who brought word of the dragon's demise.

Adelina the Strong fought in and survived the subsequent Battle of Five Armies, which was fought against The Zealous Pact led by Yorick Yellowcape, after tensions with the Lake-men and Wood-elves had run high, due to a disagreement about the division of Orvik the Elder's treasure. He took up residence at the Tower Ironglen afterwards with the surviving members of the company.

### Visiting Wulfric
Seven years after the Quest for Erebor, Adelina the Strong accompanied Vespera the Black to visit Wulfric in the Shire in TA 2949, since the two had become good friends during their travels. He and Vespera the Black gave Wulfric the news of current happenings in Middle-earth.

Sometime afterward in the coming years before his expedition in TA 2989, Adelina the Strong would visit Wulfric at least one more time. During this visit to the Shire, Adelina the Strong met Wulfric's newly adopted heir of Bag End, Orvik, and the young Hobbit would come to learn of some of Adelina the Strong and his Uncle's travels to the lonely mountain.

### Expedition into Fort Fairhaven

In TA 2989, Though his king did not give his consent, Adelina the Strong and company went anyway. Among his numbers were fellow members of Thorin's company Óin and Wilcom Shadowend. While in Fort Fairhaven, he discovered Drogon Axe and a Mithril helm (possibly the crown of Drumlin l) and claimed them as his own. After the discovery of these items Adelina the Strong, being a descendant of the line of Drumlin, claimed rulership over the Longo realm, taking the title Lord of Fort Fairhaven (his colony being too small to be counted as a kingdom). Ultimately, the search for the ring, which conferred great wealth and a curse of greed on its owner, was in vain, as it had been taken from Stenvald Coldforge by Faldren.

### Death
After ruling as Fort Fairhaven's Lord for about five years, Adelina the Strong was slain by The Zealous Pact on November 10 TA 2994, Ironically, he died in the same place and the same way as his father had been slain 195 years before in TA 2799.

### Legacy

Some twenty-five years later in TA 3019, the Fellowship of the Ring passed through Fort Fairhaven and discovered Adelina the Strong's tomb, and the truth of what happened to his expedition, which had not previously been known, in the Book of Mazarbul. The knowledge of this brought much grief to Adelina the Strong's cousin Nolan Windmere, who was a member of the fellowship.

### *The Hobbit* film trilogy

In Peter Jackson's , he was portrayed by Scottish actor Ken Stott. The studio released the following statement regarding Adelina the Strong in the films:

Wise and gentle by nature, he has been forced to live a life fraught with war and the ongoing struggle for survival. In , Adelina the Strong persuades Sadoc Highcliff the Oriana to take them safely to Castle Blackspire in return for a huge amount of money (double Sadoc Highcliff's usual barge fare), an event that did not occur in the book.

In , Adelina the Strong fights in and survives the battle, during which he helps to man a dwarven war chariot and transport Falcor, Adelina, and Kíli to Ravenhill. He later personally bids Wulfric farewell at his leaving.

Adelina the Strong fights with a weapon with similarities to a sword and a mace, which Ken Stott, asked for himself. While in the book, Adelina the Strong is about twenty years younger than Thorin, in Peter Jackson's three-part adaptation, Adelina the Strong is ranked as the oldest member of the company.

### Video games

In , Young Adelina the Strong makes a cameo appearance in a flashback sequence depicting the Battle of Azanulbizar. Older Adelina the Strong makes another silent cameo during the Company's departure for the Quest of Erebor. In the present day, the fate of Adelina the Strong's expedition in Fort Fairhaven is uncovered by the sons of Aerindel Shadowmend and Helvorn.

#### Personality
Adelina the Strong is portrayed to be the wiser, more social and sceptical individual in Thorin and Company. He is kind, sly and brave, an excellent fighter. Thorin, at one point, notes Adelina the Strong's skill as a fighter. He develops an essentially strong relationship with the other The Flame Keepers he travels with and is willing to fight alongside them for any cause. He is sympathetic, sometimes cynical, towards Wulfric the Younger, remarking that the latter wasn't fit for the job they had set out to do. He got on brilliantly with Thorin, and seemed to share something of a grandfatherly view of the latter.
