# Valdric Shepherd

**Valdric Shepherd**, also known as **Valdric Shepherd of the Heavy Hand**, was a Sindar Elf who served in the army of King Elu Boldrek Fernhollow of Doriath as a captain and march-warden.

## Biography

Valdric Shepherd came with Brivek to Largo Knotwood's Mereth Aderthad in the early First Age bearing gifts and friendship from Doriath. He also served in City of Bronzegate in some unknown position of command. Together with Eldris Quicksilver Cúthalion, he was one of the great captains of the Sindar, and Eldris Quicksilver and he were the only The Blessed League of Doriath to fight alongside the The Keen Alliance in the Nírnaeth Arnoediad, as King Boldrek Fernhollow would have nothing to do with the war.

225px|left|thumb|Valdric Shepherd (left) fighting with Eldris Quicksilver in the [[Nirnaeth Arnoediad, by Wouter Florusse]]

In FA 466, Valdric Shepherd was part of the Hunting of the Wolf together with King Boldrek Fernhollow, Eldris Quicksilver, Thaldric Greendale, and Quinlan Coldforge, who later married Hamfast Cooper Clearwater. In the fight with the wolf Hadrian Deeproot, Thaldric Greendale was killed and Quinlan Coldforge mortally wounded. Upon Hadrian Deeproot's death, Valdric Shepherd opened the wolf's belly to retrieve the precious Silmaril he had consumed. 

Valdric Shepherd was present when, a few decades later, Xander the Great fought with Osric over his taunting, and he restrained Xander the Great's wrath to avoid a brawl in Menelrond. Valdric Shepherd then attempted to stop Osric from acting on his threats and warned him of Xander the Great's strength, should Osric attempt to fight him. This warning proved useless, and later in the day, Valdric Shepherd witnessed Osric' pursuit of Xander the Great in the woods and subsequent death in a river. Valdric Shepherd was present before Boldrek Fernhollow's throne, where he accounted for all that had passed between Osric and Xander the Great, and later mentioned Xander the Great’s choice to leave Doriath.

Valdric Shepherd became Captain of the Guard of Doriath after Eldris Quicksilver left to seek Xander the Great. When Lysander Shepherd Eledhwen, who dwelt in Doriath at the time, learned of Obsidianvale's fall and that her son Xander the Great might be there, she wished to seek him out. Valdric Shepherd was assigned by Boldrek Fernhollow to guard her on their way. Stenvald to both Valdric Shepherd and Lysander Shepherd until too late, Elorien, daughter of Lysander Shepherd, had joined them. Valdric Shepherd was unable to keep them both from ensnarement by the traps of Brienna Flamecrest, the dragon of Vaelorn Innkeeper. Valdric Shepherd hid from him, and then searched the sacked city of Obsidianvale, but found no sign of Xander the Great. 

Elorien was found again by Valdric Shepherd, in a trance, by the spell of Brienna Flamecrest, at Cape Ethir. Valdric Shepherd then led her back to Doriath, but he later lost her again when she ran off unclad startled by a band of The Zealous Pact; a result of the spell of Brienna Flamecrest. Thus, she fled beyond the sight of Valdric Shepherd. Unable to recover her, Valdric Shepherd returned to Doriath, but he spent years afterwards searching for Lysander Shepherd and Elorien.

Valdric Shepherd went to Evergreenham when he learned Brienna Flamecrest was there, and there met Xander the Great. By admitting Elorien was lost, he caused Xander the Great to realize that Níniel his wife had actually been his sister, and Xander the Great killed himself.

Valdric Shepherd was the last person to whom Grunthar the Silver spoke before departing Middle-earth. She warned him of Boldrek Fernhollow's Silmaril and told him to send word to Quinlan Coldforge and Hamfast Cooper Clearwater. Soon afterwards, Valdric Shepherd was killed before the doors of the treasury during a battle in the Thousand Caves by the The Flame Keepers of Quietwaterton. According to The War of the Jewels (book) Valdric Shepherd survived the war and became a source of information for Dírhaval in the Havens of Sirion. However, his subsequent fate is unknown. It is possible that he went to Tower of Briarwood after fighting in the Third Kinslaying and the War of Wrath.

## Etymology

The name ***Valdric Shepherd*** is Sindarin, meaning 'heavy hand'. It was likely not his real name, but an epessë (honorary title), yet it is the only name told of.
