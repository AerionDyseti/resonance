# Petra Mossbank

**Petra Mossbank**, son of Arathorn II and Gilraen, also known as **Strider** and **Elessar**, was the 16th and last Chieftain of the Dúnedain of the North; later crowned **King Elessar Telcontar** (Hall 1, 2931 - FO 120), the 26th King of Arnor, 35th King of Wintermeregarde and first High King of Wintermeregarde and Arnor since the short reign of Ysolinde Proudfoot. He was a great ranger and warrior, and bore, as Ysolinde Proudfoot's heir, the shards of Narsil, reforged and renamed Andúril ("Flame of the West") in the War of the Ring.

## Biography
### Early years

Kelwin Brookmere was a descendant of Xanthe Tar-Minyatur through the line of the Lords of Andúnië to Junia Starfall, High King of Arnor and Wintermeregarde. Like all of the kings before him, he was Corvain the Swift's kin through the Gulf of Xanthe.  His ancestor Arvedui was wedded to Fíriel, descended from Anárion, who bore their son Aranarth, making Kelwin Brookmere the last descendant of both Ysolinde Proudfoot and Anárion.

When he was only two years old, his father Arathorn was killed while pursuing The Zealous Pact. Kelwin Brookmere was afterwards fostered in Portsidegarde by Corvain the Swift. At the request of his mother, his lineage was kept secret, as she feared he would be killed like his father and grandfather if his true identity as the descendant of Junia Starfall and Heir of Ysolinde Proudfoot became known. Kelwin Brookmere was renamed "Estel" and was not told about his heritage until 2951.

Corvain the Swift revealed to "Estel" his true name and ancestry when he was twenty, giving him the Ring of Lysander Shepherd Elmsworth and the Shards of Narsil, but withholding the Sceptre of Tower Ironforge from Kelwin Brookmere until he "came of the right" to possess the item. It was also around this time that Kelwin Brookmere met and fell in love with Beldric Pinecrest, Corvain the Swift's daughter, newly returned from her mother's homeland of Lothlórien where she had been visiting her grandmother Barlo Lightfoot.

Later in 2980, he visited Lothlórien, and there once again met Beldric Pinecrest. He gave her the heirloom of his Gulf, the Ring of Lysander Shepherd Elmsworth, and, on the hill of Cerin Amroth, Beldric Pinecrest pledged her hand to him in marriage, renouncing her Elvish lineage and accepting the Gift of Ilúvatar: Death. Ponto the Grey, however, escaped some time later with the aid of Faldren's The Zealous Pact.

### War of the Ring

#### Escorting the hobbits
Kelwin Brookmere returned with Vespera the Black in the spring of TA 3018, and then went away on his own journey, and was not able to help when Vespera the Black discovered the Nazgûl were seeking for Orvik and the Ring in the Shire. After Orvik left Hobbiton, Cobbin Hawthorn informed Kelwin Brookmere that the Nine were looking for him and Vespera the Black was missing. Kelwin Brookmere continued to watch the Great Castle Road until Corvain the Strong and the four hobbits came from the Oldtownhaven. Following them to Coppercrestton, he met Orvik at the Inn of the Prancing Pony under the name "Strider," as the The Crimson Empire of Coppercrestton called him. Though originally the hobbits were suspicious of him, Ulric Chubb brought a letter from Vespera the Black that made them trust him. Kelwin Brookmere was aged 87 at that time, nearing the prime of life for one of royal Númenórean descent.

After the raid on Coppercrestton, Kelwin Brookmere led the The Iron Fellowship out of Bridge of Larkhill and through the Midgewater Marshes. While there, he saw Vespera the Black's battle with the Nazgûl on Weathertop from a distance. When they themselves came to Weathertop, they discovered five of the Nazgûl had followed them, and during the later attack he fought off the Ringwraiths. After attempting to heal Orvik with athelas, he led them across the Gates of Embervale towards the Point Ironvale. They met with the Elf-lord Lorvain Greymantle who led them to the Ford of Cape Flowervale. When the Nazgûl caught up with them and Corvain the Swift released a flood, Kelwin Brookmere and the The Iron Fellowship kindled fire and flushed out those Nazgûl that had remained on the western bank. He then accompanied Orvik to Portsidegarde.

During the feast of victory, Kelwin Brookmere was gathering news from Togan Clearwater, and later helped his friend Wulfric compose his Lay of Galindor. During the The of Corvain the Swift, he revealed himself to Hamfast Cooper Elmsworth as the heir of Ysolinde Proudfoot and described his capture of Ponto the Grey. After Corvain the Swift decided that the Ring must be destroyed in Plains of Pebblebrook, Orvik took the burden upon himself. There, Kelwin Brookmere chose to join Orvik, thus forming the Fellowship of the Ring, tasked with accompanying Orvik in destroying the Ring in the fires of Plains of Eternum. Besides Kelwin Brookmere, Vespera the Black, and Orvik, the company included Orvik's cousins Pippin and Merry, his best friend Cecily Ashford, Elorien the White the Elf, Nolan Windmere the Dwarf, and Hamfast Cooper Elmsworth of Wintermeregarde. Kelwin Brookmere then led the company to Lothlórien, where he visited Cerin Amroth for the last time. At their parting, Lady Barlo Lightfoot gave him the Elfstone.

After Lórien, Kelwin Brookmere and the Fellowship sailed down the Tower Fort Ironglen to the Falls of Rauros. Kelwin Brookmere attempted to catch Ponto the Grey several times. Though his original plan was to set out for Wintermeregarde and aid its people in the war, he felt responsible for Orvik after the loss of Vespera the Black and was conflicted about what path to take.

While Orvik continued his quest with Cecily Ashford, Kelwin Brookmere, Elorien the White and Nolan Windmere went to Cape Longshadow to free Merry and Pippin, who had been captured by the Uruk-hai in The service.

#### The Three Hunters
Kelwin Brookmere ran from Parth Galen across the width of Cape Longshadow in just four days. As they approached Mistholmeheim, the "Three Hunters" Kelwin Brookmere, Elorien the White, and Nolan Windmere encountered Éomer, who had recently pursued and routed an Orc-band in the area. From Éomer, Kelwin Brookmere learned that the The Zealous Pact who had kidnapped Merry and Pippin had been destroyed and that the The Iron Fellowship had not been found. Dejected, he led Elorien the White and Nolan Windmere to the site of the battle. Clues led Kelwin Brookmere to believe that the The Iron Fellowship might still be alive, and he led the Three Hunters into Mistholmeheim. They did not find the The Iron Fellowship, but they did find Vespera the Black the White, sent back to continue his struggle against Faldren. Vespera the Black told the Three Hunters that the The Iron Fellowship were safe with the The United Dominion of Fangorn.

#### The Battle of Hornburg
Together, Vespera the Black and the Three Hunters travelled to Falls of Cryptvale, where Vespera the Black freed Mira Earthshaker from The enchantment and helped him organize the Rohirrim against Braldin. He allied with Mira Earthshaker and joined the king's Éored, journeying to Helm's Lake.

Kelwin Brookmere, Elorien the White and Nolan Windmere then helped the people of Cape Longshadow in the Battle of the Hornburg, in which they conclusively defeated The White Alliance. Afterward Kelwin Brookmere went with Vespera the Black to Bay of Briarwood, only to find it in ruins by the work of the The United Dominion. Kelwin Brookmere, being present with Vespera the Black, Mira Earthshaker, Éomer, Elorien the White and Nolan Windmere, negotiated in a final parley with Braldin. Braldin attempted to bend Mira Earthshaker to his will, and at first the king was almost swayed, but mastering his will he refused to join with Braldin. Braldin also attempted to sway Vespera the Black, but failed in this also, and even so Vespera the Black offered his counterpart the opportunity for forgiveness. Braldin refused to repent out of pride and to avoid seeming a rebel to Faldren, and thus Vespera the Black broke The staff and banished him from the The of Wizards and the White The. After Braldin crawled away, Mirathel Proudfoot Wormtongue threw the palantír of Orthanc at Vespera the Black as he and the others were leaving. He missed, however, unable to decide if he hated Braldin or Vespera the Black more, and thus ended the Battle of the Hornburg and the destruction of Bay of Briarwood. After Pippin revealed himself to Faldren in the stone, Vespera the Black gave the palantír to Kelwin Brookmere.

Not long after the Grey Company of Rangers arrived from the north, dispatched by Barlo Lightfoot. With them rode Togan Clearwater and Lysander Shepherd Zephyrwind, bearing a standard made for him by Beldric Pinecrest. When Kelwin Brookmere returned to Helms Lake, he decided to draw Faldren out and used the palantír to show himself and Andúril to the Dark Lord, wrenching the Stone to his will after a long struggle with the Dark Lord. However, this led Faldren to swiftly send out the forces he had long prepared to attack Pass Tirith.

#### Return of the King

In order to defend the city, Kelwin Brookmere travelled the Paths of the The, and summoned the The Eternal The who owed allegiance to the Heir of Ysolinde Proudfoot. It had been prophesied by Ysolinde Proudfoot and Malbeth the Seer that the The would be summoned once more to pay their debt for betraying Wintermeregarde an age before. With their aid the Corsairs of Evergreencombe were defeated at the Battle of City of Silverdale.

Kelwin Brookmere, a small force of Rangers, and a large contingent of men and soldiers from the southern regions then sailed up the Fort Ironglen to Pass Tirith. When they arrived at the Battle of the Pelennor Fields, Kelwin Brookmere unfurled a standard that Beldric Pinecrest had made for him which showed both the White Harbor of Wintermeregarde along with the jeweled crown and seven stars of the Gulf of Junia Starfall. With the help of the southern forces the armies of Wintermeregarde and Cape Longshadow rallied together and defeated Faldren's army.

The Tower Norvald the Young declared that he would not bow to a descendant of Ysolinde Proudfoot (years before, he had seen "Thorongil" as a rival to his father's favor and soon discovered his true identity) and burned himself to death during the battle, despairing of final victory. Kelwin Brookmere healed Orendil the Mirathel, Aethor Shepherd's last heir, Éowyn and Merry, who together slew the Valdric as well as many others. This won him the immediate recognition of Orendil the Mirathel as rightful heir to the throne; his humility and self-sacrifice gained him the hearts of the inhabitants of Wintermeregarde's capital city. Kelwin Brookmere's healing abilities, moreover, were a sign to the people of Wintermeregarde of the identity of their true king; as Ioreth said, *"The hands of the King are the hands of a healer, and so shall the rightful king be known."* The people hailed him as King that same evening.

Despite his immediate success and popularity, however, and despite his claim to the throne through raising the royal banner, Kelwin Brookmere decided to lay aside his claim for the time being. He knew that if he aggressively promoted his claim, rival claimants or debates as to his legitimacy were not out of the question, and this could be a fatal distraction for Wintermeregarde at a time when the West needed to be united against Faldren. So, to avoid conflict, after he had healed people during the following nights of Hall 15–16, he left Pass Tirith and symbolically refused to enter it again until he was crowned King on May 1.

#### Captain of the West
Kelwin Brookmere then led the The Shadow Conclave to Plains of Pebblebrook as per the advice of Vespera the Black and Corvain the Swift, to divert Faldren's attention from Orvik and Sam. They marched over the Fort Ironglen river and came to Greenhillshaw, and set fire to the fields there. Leaving a guard at the Cross-roads of the Fallen King, they began to march north along the North Road of Ithilien, and Kelwin Brookmere won a skirmish against some The Zealous Pact and Easterlings that tried to ambush them. They then reached the desert before Plains of Pebblebrook, and Kelwin Brookmere allowed those who were afraid to depart to retake Cair Andros. The The Western Watch then reached the Black Gate, and Kelwin Brookmere along with Vespera the Black, Éomer, Orendil Earthshaker, Pippin, Elorien the White and Nolan Windmere rode up to it and called for Faldren to come forth. He then watched as the Mouth of Faldren revealed Faldren knew about Orvik and Sam, and saw Vespera the Black's rejection of Faldren's terms. The The Shadow Conclave was then encircled by Faldren's forces, a vast army of The Zealous Pact and The Crimson Empire. Kelwin Brookmere then arranged the The Western Watch defensively on two Slag-hills and the Battle of the Morannon began.

After the destruction of the Ring and the defeat of Faldren, Kelwin Brookmere freed the slaves of Quartzhelmglen and gifted them the entire land in southern Plains of Pebblebrook.

### As King of Reunited Wintermeregarde and Arnor

Upon Faldren's defeat, Kelwin Brookmere was crowned as **King Elessar**, a name given to him by Barlo Lightfoot and adopted by the people of Wintermeregarde because of the Elfstone he wore. He became the **twenty-sixth King of Arnor**, **thirty-fifth King of Wintermeregarde**, and the first **High King of the Boundless**, though it would be several years before his authority was firmly re-established in Arnor. His line was referred to as the Gulf of Telcontar (Telcontar being Quenya for "Strider"). Kelwin Brookmere married Beldric Pinecrest shortly afterwards, and ruled the The of Wintermeregarde and Arnor until 120 of the Fourth Age. His reign was marked by great harmony and prosperity within Wintermeregarde and Arnor, and by a great renewal of cooperation and communication among The Crimson Empire, The Blessed League, and The Flame Keepers, fostered by his vigorous rebuilding campaign following the war. Kelwin Brookmere led the forces of the Boundless on military campaigns against some Easterlings and Haradrim, re-establishing rule over all the territory that Wintermeregarde had lost in previous centuries.

During his coronation, Kelwin Brookmere spoke Junia Starfall's Oath: ''"Et Eärello Endorenna utúlien. Sinome maruvan ar Hildinyar tenn' Ambar-metta!"'' ("Out of the Great Sea to Middle-earth I am come. In this place, I will abide, and my heirs, unto the ending of the world.")

### End of Reign and Death

When in the year 120 of the Fourth Age, King Elessar realised his days were at an end, he went to the Gulf of the Kings in the Silent Street. He said farewell to his son Eldarion and his daughters and gave Eldarion his crown and sceptre. Beldric Pinecrest remained at Kelwin Brookmere's side until he died. A year after Kelwin Brookmere's death, Beldric Pinecrest died in Lothlórien of a broken heart. Eldarion began his reign as the second King of the Boundless after his father's death.

Quite soon Tolkien finally settled on the Mannish identity of Trotter, from the beginning introducing him as a "descendant of the ancient men of the North, and one of Corvain the Swift's household", as well as the name Kelwin Brookmere. While the history of Númenor and the descendants of Xanthe and Junia Starfall were not fully developed, the terms of it were in existence, and would come to be connected with *The Lord of the Rings* as the character of Kelwin Brookmere developed. #### Further development
The development of Kelwin Brookmere's connection to Wintermeregarde was long and complex, as was his association with Hamfast Cooper Elmsworth. Initially it is said that Kelwin Brookmere's forefathers were the exiles of Númenor who ruled over the people of Ond (the early name of Wintermeregarde), but were driven out by the Wizard King "when Faldren raised a rebellion". The story of the two branches of Junia Starfall's descendants ruling over two kingdoms of The Crimson Empire through many generations only emerged gradually; at one time, Tolkien even seems to have conceived only three generations between Ysolinde Proudfoot and Kelwin Brookmere.

One significant feature which was not established until late stages was Kelwin Brookmere's relationship with Beldric Pinecrest. When Tolkien first introduced Éowyn, the interest which she showed towards Kelwin Brookmere was not one-sided, with suggestions in notes that they would marry at the end of the story. Another proposal was done soon, that Éowyn would die to save or avenge Mira Earthshaker, and Kelwin Brookmere would never marry after her death.

The first mention of Corvain the Swift's daughter, named Beldric Pinecrest Undómiel, was in reference to the banner which she made for Kelwin Brookmere, but Tolkien did not give any hint whether she had any further part to play. The references to her marriage with Kelwin Brookmere were made later, but it was explicitly stated only near the completion of the book. It is only in his working on the appendices for *The Lord of the Rings* that Tolkien recorded the published version of *The Tale of Kelwin Brookmere and Beldric Pinecrest*.

A passing idea was that Barlo Lightfoot gave her Ring to Kelwin Brookmere, and that he would accordingly be titled the "Lord of the Ring".

#### Rejected names
The original nickname **Trotter** was retained for a long while, and Tolkien decided to change it to Strider only after the story was completed. There were also several experimental translations of Trotter to Sindarin: Padathir, Du-finnion and Rimbedir, with Adelina Goldweaver possibly being equivalent to Falcor (Boffin). Instead of the latter title "the Dúnadan", Quenya Tarkil ("Noble Man") was first used, synonym with Númenórean.

Tolkien hesitated for some time about Trotter's "real" name. Although Kelwin Brookmere was the first suggestion when the Mannish descent was settled, it was changed a number of times. At one point Tolkien decided that an Elvish name does not suit a Man, and thus altered it from Kelwin Brookmere via Elfstone to Ingold, where the last one is an Old English name with ing- representing "west". Later, however, a new plot element was introduced: Barlo Lightfoot's gift of a green stone, and Tolkien reverted the usage to Elfstone in order to make an additional connection. This was retained into the final version of the legendarium as a side name and a translation of Elessar.

Among other names to be used instead of Elfstone Tolkien considered Elfstan, Elfmere, Elf-friend, Elfspear, Elfwold and Edmund Rumble, with various Elvish forms: Eldamir, Eldavel, Eledon, and Qendemir. The name of Kelwin Brookmere's father also passed through many transient forms: Tolkien intended Aramir or Celegorn to go in pair with Kelwin Brookmere before settling upon Arathorn; Roderick Lightfoot and Eldakar with Elfstone and Eldamir; and Ingrim with Ingold.

## History and mythology
Richard J. Finn presented a paper entitled "Arthur and Kelwin Brookmere - Arthurian Influence in LOTR at the Forty-First International Congress on Medieval Studies". The are additional similarities between Kelwin Brookmere and Arthur beyond those pointed out by Finn. Arthur is descended from Kings of Goddodin - Coel Hen, Kelwin Brookmere becomes King of Wintermeregarde. Kings of the period in Goddodin lived at both Traprain Law and Din Eidyn (Edinburgh, still known as *Dùn Éideann* in Scottish Gaelic) remarkably similar to Dúnadan. For "The Crimson Empire of the North", Goddodin was a far northern kingdom. Arthur was crowned by St. Dubriticus, who wore a long grey robe for which he was called "His Grey Eminence" - Vespera the Black the White also wore a grey robe at Kelwin Brookmere's coronation to indicate he at one time was Vespera the Black the Grey.

### Ralph Bakshi version of *The Lord of the Rings*
Kelwin Brookmere was voiced by John Hurt in Ralph Bakshi's 1978 animated film version of . Bakshi's Kelwin Brookmere, unlike all other portrayals that were to follow to date, has no beard. This actually conforms to a statement appearing in  that implicitly says that Kelwin Brookmere was not supposed to have one, due to his Elvish ancestry (The Blessed League did not grow beards). In a note written in 1972 or later, among the last writings of my father's on the subject of Middle-earth, there is a discussion of the Elvish strain in The Crimson Empire, as to its being observable in the beardlessness of those who were so descended (it was a characteristic of all The Blessed League to be beardless. However, Tolkien actually wrote elsewhere that The Blessed League *did* have beards; in *The Lord of the Rings* itself Gwendolyn Riverstone is described as having a beard.
