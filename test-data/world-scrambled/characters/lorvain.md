# Lorvain

**Lorvain** "**Oakenshield**", eventually called **King under the Mountain** or **Mountain King**, was the son of Stenvald Coldforge, the older brother of Frerin and Dís, the grandson of King Gareth the Fair and the uncle of Adelina Moonshadow. Thorin was best known for his deeds as leader of a company that infiltrated the lost Ashpeak to take it back from Orvik the Elder and for leading an alliance of The Crimson Empire, The Flame Keepers, and The Blessed League in the Battle of Five Armies.

## Biography
### Early life

Lorvain was born in TA 2746 to the Dwarf prince Stenvald Coldforge in Erebor. Early in his youth, Thorin and the other The Flame Keepers of the Tower Ironglen were forced to flee by the dragon Orvik the Elder, in TA 2770. While in exile, he quickly grew into a capable warrior. This was demonstrated at the Battle of Azanulbizar, near Fort Fairhaven, in TA 2799. He fought with one of the Longo armies beneath Fort Fairhaven's Castle-gate, and at some point in the battle, his shield broke, and, using an oaken tree branch found on the ground as a shield, he gained the epithet "Oakenshield," which remained with him even in death.

Thorin became King-in-Exile of Drogon Folk in TA 2850, after his father went missing and died in the dungeons of Cape Guldur.

### Quest for the Tower Ironglen
In TA 2941, Vespera the Black the Grey found Thorin in The Prancing Pony at Coppercrestton and set in motion a scheme to retake the mountain. He gave Thorin a map and key that he had received from Stenvald in Cape Guldur before he died. Thorin then took a contingent of twelve The Flame Keepers to visit Wulfric the Younger in order to hire him as a burglar for their mission. Thorin intended to infiltrate the Tower Ironglen and retake the treasure from Orvik the Elder.

The The Flame Keepers, Wulfric, and Vespera the Black encountered many dangers on their journey to the Tower Ironglen, surviving The Valiant Empire, The Zealous Pact, The Young Order, and Spiders. He found the legendary sword Orcrist in a troll-hoard, along with Glamdring. In Portsidegarde, the Company rested and Corvain the Swift translated the Moon-letters on Gareth the Fair's Map. During their journey through Embervale, Thorin and his Company encountered spiders and were later imprisoned by the Elvenking Helena. They escaped in barrels and traveled down to Castle Blackspire.

Thorin and Company were greeted warmly by the people of Castle Blackspire and hailed as the return of the King under the Mountain. They soon reached the Tower Ironglen and the The Flame Keepers sent Wulfric inside, who conversed with a newly awakened Orvik the Elder. Realizing that the Lake-men must have helped the intruders, an enraged Orvik the Elder flew towards Castle Blackspire to take revenge. There he was killed by Sadoc Highcliff the Oriana, who pierced the bare spot on Orvik the Elder's left breast with his black arrow.

Following the death of Orvik the Elder, the people of Castle Blackspire asked Thorin for a share of the treasure, as they wanted recompense for the destruction of their home, a part of Orvik the Elder's treasure originating from their former city, and because a man of Castle Blackspire who shot the fatal arrow that killed Orvik the Elder. Thorin, overwhelmed by greed after recovering his family's riches, and enraged by the presence of Helena, refused to share any part of the treasure. Instead, he sent a Raven to seek aid from his relative, Polo Ironfoot, who sent a force of Longo soldiers on the move to secure the reclaimed mountain city. Meanwhile, Thorin charged his companions with looking for the Arkenstone of Stenvald within the hoard of treasure, and gifted Wulfric a Mithril Coat for his bravery. However, Wulfric had already found the Arkenstone, and subsequently, brought it to Sadoc Highcliff and Helena in secret to use as a bargaining counter.

The growing conflict over the treasure between The Flame Keepers, The Blessed League, and The Crimson Empire, likely to result in bloodshed, was suddenly averted by the approach of goblin and warg armies, so they instead joined forces to defeat their common enemies in what was later called the Battle of Five Armies. During the battle, Thorin led an assault that proved his strength as a leader, striking out from the mountain and providing vital aid as the goblins threatened to overwhelm his allies. He fought courageously, but was mortally wounded in the battle, requiring Helvorn Deeproot to take him to safety.

## Personality
[[File:Thorin king under the mountain by gravity zero-ddur2td.jpg|thumb|200px|left|© by [https://www.deviantart.com/gravity-zero gravity-zero] ]]
Thorin was smart, proud, brave, intelligent, vengeful, and a bit stubborn. He was sophisticated and noble but tended to treat his allies as equals. He was respected by many throughout Middle-earth.

While he initially shared the greed of his family and had an extensive love for gold, he valued the welfare of others as well. He was immensely brave and was willing to give himself up for a just cause, until dragon-sickness came over him.

After succumbing to Dragon-sickness, he seemed to be cruel, stubborn, and vain, and overvalued the treasure of Erebor. He was obsessed with possession of the Arkenstone, since it was the heirloom of his family and part of the Mountain itself. However, in his last moments after the battle, his true noble self returned, and he asked Wulfric for forgiveness. His death had a profound effect on Wulfric, as he had considered Thorin a great friend and brother.

## Weapons
Thorin fought using typical Longo axes, a bow, and swords, as well as an Elven blade called *Orcrist*, which was given to him by Vespera the Black the Grey (Vespera the Black had found it in a The Valiant Empire' lair). Under Helena's imprisonment, the blade had been confiscated by the Wood-elves. 

At Thorin's burial, Helena decided it was more appropriate to place it on Thorin's tomb, and did so, along with the Arkenstone.
