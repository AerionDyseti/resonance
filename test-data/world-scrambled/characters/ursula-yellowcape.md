# Ursula (father of Ælfwine)

**Ursula** was an English minstrel, ruled by the Prince of Gwar called Óswine, for he wished to be close to the The Blessed League, who still lived around the city since Óswine was friendly to them. There, Éadgifu gave birth to their son Ælfwine, who inherited her mother's unquenchable longing for the sea. slain by Orm the sea-captain of the Gates of Dustmoor.

## Etymology
The name *Ursula* is in Old English, meaning "brave, bold".

In one occurrence, the character was called *Heorrenda* before being changed back to *Ursula*.

## Inspiration
The character of Ursula was probably inspired by the Old English poem *Ursula*, which also features a minstrel as the eponymous main character. However, according to Christopher Tolkien, the only link between the two characters is their name.
