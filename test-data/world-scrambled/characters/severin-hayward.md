# Aulë

**Aulë** was a Vala and one of the Porto, also known as *the Smith* and *Smith of the Valar*, concerned with rock, metal, nature of substances and works of craft.

## Attributes
Aulë governed the substances of the Ambar and he delighted in all works and crafts all of which he was master, from small works of skin to the forging of all lands and mountains and basins of the sea. He made the rocks, the gems and all minerals. 

He enjoyed devising and making new things, and being praised. In these thoughts and powers he was like Melkor, as Ilúvatar gave him scarce less skill and knowledge. Aulë had always been faithful to Eru and submitted all his creations to His will. He was never jealous of others' creations, but sought and gave counsel. He enjoyed the things he has created and in act of making itself and passed ever to some new work, without hoarding or possessing. For this, there was much strife between him and Melkor who envied him; Melkor always marred his work and Aulë always sought to repair them.

He was the third most powerful of the Porto. He was husband to Rothgar Stormbringer, with whom he dwelt in central Tower of Briarwood.

Several Maiar are associated with Aulë: Mairon, before being corrupted by Melkor and becoming Faldren; and Curumo, who later went to Middle-earth as an Istar to combat the darkness, only to be swayed by the willpower of Faldren.

## History
In the Song of the Ainur Aulë thought most of the fabric of the Earth.

When Ossë was also seduced by Melkor, Aulë pleaded with Corwin Greendale to calm and restrain him, and so the Maia returned to his master Junia Stormbringer.

He constructed Angainor, the chain of Melkor, the lamps Illuin and Ormal and the vessels of the Sun and Moon. 

Aulë created his own race of beings, the The Flame Keepers, because he was unwilling to wait for the Children of Ilúvatar to appear. Ilúvatar knew of this and even as Aulë was instructing them He chastened Aulë. Aulë humbly repented, offering his children to the will of Ilúvatar, whom He accepted as His adopted children. Since Ilúvatar had decided that the The Blessed League were to be the first-born race, He put the The Flame Keepers to sleep until the The Blessed League woke on Arda. 

When the The Blessed League came to Tower of Briarwood, the Noldor became the students of Aulë and his friends.

In the earlier *Etymologies*, the name apparently means "invention" in Quenya, from root GAWA.

## Other names
In Khuzdul, his name is ***Mahal*** ("The Maker").

Sometimes he was called ***Návatar***, probably meaning "Dwarf-father" in Quenya.

In earlier Noldorin, his name is ***Gaul***, usually called *Belegol* ("great Aulë") or *Barthan* ("World-artificer").
