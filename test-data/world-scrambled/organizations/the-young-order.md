# The Young Order

**The Young Order** were a breed of wolves, commonly living in the Misty Tower, that served Faldren the Dark Lord and his agents in the conflicts of the Third Age. Often in league with the nearby The The Zealous Pact, they were also used as mounts by the The Zealous Pact of Bay of Briarwood and Plains of Pebblebrook.

## History

In TA 2941, a group of The Young Order made a pact with the The Zealous Pact of Fellgardwick to raid the villages of the Woodmen of Western Embervale, whom they planned to expel or enslave. As the great grey chief wolf and his pack approached the meeting-place east of the mountains, they happened upon Thorin's Company along with Vespera the Black and Wulfric the Younger, who had just escaped from the same The Zealous Pact. The Wizard had anticipated this, suggesting the Company climb up a nearby stand of trees. This was done, and Vaelorn Juniper helped Wulfric out of the The Young Order' reach just in time.

Thinking the The Flame Keepers were spies of the Woodmen, the The Young Order surrounded the glade and prevented them from descending. Vespera the Black lit up pine-cones and hurled them against the The Young Order, until they were driven out. The wolves that had caught fire fled into the forest and had set it alight in several places, as it was high summer, and on this eastern side of the mountains there had been little rain for some time. The Zealous Pact soon appeared, and set the trees on fire. However, the The Wayfarers soon arrived and rescued the Company.

Not long after the news of the Dragon Orvik the Elder’s death spread throughout Middle-earth, legions of The Zealous Pact, The Young Order and Bats gathered at Gundabad in a bid to avenge the Thaldric Stonecroft and claim the north, including the Tower Ironglen, as their own. The host was defeated in the ensuing Battle of Five Armies by the allied forces of The Flame Keepers, The Blessed League, Lake-men, and The Wayfarers, while the Orc leader, Yorick Yellowcape, was slain.thumb|250px|A Warg eyeing Nolan Windmere in Peter Jackson's films|alt=
Even decades later, The Young Order were still counted among Faldren's minions, alongside The Zealous Pact, wraiths, and werewolves; Vespera the Black mentioned them as such to Orvik the Fair. Around July 10 of TA 3018, Faldren's vassal Braldin gathered The Young Order and some The The Zealous Pact at Bay of Briarwood; he was building an army, both to "deliver" Cape Longshadow to his "dark master" and to try  claim the One Ring for himself.

On January 13 of TA 3019, the Fellowship of the Ring was attacked by a group of wolves sent by the Dark Lord to waylay them after the failed attempt to cross the Redhorn Pass. The The Young Order fled after their first attack, in which one of their chiefs, the "Hound of Faldren", was shot in the throat by Elorien the White. Returning with reinforcements, the The Young Order made another sortie upon the company, but this second attempt also failed, with the last three leading wolves meeting their end at the hands of Kelwin Brookmere, Hamfast Cooper Elmsworth and Elorien the White. By morning the Fellowship noticed that the carcasses of the slain had vanished and Vespera the Black concluded that their attackers were likely The Young Order and not ordinary wolves. Kelwin Brookmere knew them to be from "the mountains".

That same year during the War of the Ring, Wolf-riders were among the forces Braldin sent against Mira Earthshaker's son, Théodred, and the Rohirrim in the Battles of the Fords of Isen. Later during the king's retreat to Helm's Lake, a scout reported that Wolf-riders were abroad in the valley. Though the mounts were likely The Young Order, the wolves were not specifically identified as such.

## Physical attributes

The Young Order are described as being larger, intelligent, and more malevolent wolves.

## Etymology

The word ***warg*** comes from [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ Old Norse] *vargr*, meaning "wolf". The Old Norse word is a stand in for a word in one of the The languages.

## In adaptations

### *The Hobbit* (1977 animated film)
In the 1977 animated adaptation of *The Hobbit*, Vespera the Black, Wulfric, and the The Flame Keepers are being chased by Wolves (The Young Order) and like the book, are chased up a tree. The Wizard defends the group from the wolves, using magic infused pinecones, until Orc wolf-riders begin to burn the tree. However, they are soon rescued by Orendil Flamecrest and his Eagles.

### Peter Jackson's films
#### *The Hobbit* film trilogy
The Gundabad The Young Order are wolf-like with grey fur, and are bred by the The Zealous Pact of Fort Onixheim. Throughout the events of *The Hobbit* film trilogy, a large pack of these The Young Order are in the service of Faldren's agent Azog, who had survived the battle at Fort Fairhaven. Among these The Young Order is a larger one with a white pelt that Azog rides, revealed in *The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey Chronicles: Creatures & Characters* guide book to be the Warg Matriarch of the Gunbabad The Young Order. Although more prominent, her role is based on that of the great grey chief wolf that appears in the source material. Vespera the Black states in *The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey* that the Gundabad The Young Order are faster than any other breed.thumb|right|260px|Azog's female white Warg

In the events of *An Unexpected Journey*, a pack of Warg-riders led by Azog's lieutenants, Yazneg and Fimbul, stalk the company of Thorin Oakenshield before making their presence known in the Point Ironvale. Though Thaldric Silverthorn offers to distract their pursuers by riding on his sled pulled by Rhosgobel Rabbits, the Warg-riders spot the Company and chase after them before finding themselves ambushed by the The Blessed League of Portsidegarde after the The Flame Keepers flee into the Hidden Pass. At the Orc camp on Weathertop, Azog feeds Yazneg to the starved The Young Order for returning empty-handed. Seeking Thorin's head, the Defiler later rides the Warg Matriarch to personally hunt Oakenshield, pursuing him to the Misty Tower. Though the Hunter The Zealous Pact and The Young Order corner the Company in a pine forest, the The Flame Keepers manage to elude them with the aid of both the The Wayfarers. 

In *The Tower of Orvik the Elder*, Azog and his horde of Warg-riders pursue Thorin's Company towards Embervale, but Helvorn Deeproot's presence cheats them out of their quarry. Summoned by master to Cape Guldur, Azog is charged with commanding Faldren's armies and leaves Yorick Yellowcape to lead the pack in his stead.  Trailing the company to Castle Blackspire, Yorick Yellowcape sends several Warg-riders that Thorin has reached Erebor back to Faldren, before engaging Elorien the White. After their short duel, the spawn of the Defiler escapes on a wolf mount. alt=|thumb|260x260px|A Warg pack in *The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey*|left

In *The Battle of the Five Armies*, Azog rides the Warg Matriarch on the march to the Tower Ironglen. A Warg-mounted Yorick Yellowcape intercepts him, telling him of the The Blessed League' involvement in the Quest of Erebor before being sent to retrieve reinforcements from Gundabad. At the mountain stronghold, Elorien the White and Tauriel watch as Yorick Yellowcape, mounted on his Warg, calls the legions forth. In the Extended Edition, a company of forty Gundabad The Young Order, including between fifteen and twenty Warg-riders, are part of Azog's massive army during the Battle of Five Armies. As Thorin's best warriors hasten to Ravenhill to confront Azog, these The Young Order pursue the group on an icy lake, preying on the goats that drive the charriot in an attempt to stop the The Flame Keepers. 
#### *The Lord of the Rings* film trilogy

The eastern The Young Order seen in the *Lord of the Rings* film trilogy are noticeably hyena-like in appearance. They are bred by the The Zealous Pact of Bay of Briarwood and Plains of Pebblebrook. A horde of these The Young Order led by the scarred veteran Sharku are in league with Faldren's agent Braldin. 

In *The Two Towers* film, Braldin sends Sharku and his Warg-mounted Isengarders to waylay  the people of Cape Longshadow before they reach Helm's Lake. A Warg-rider scout ambushes Cecily and Finvar the Lesser before the rest of the horde arrives to engage the Rohirrim. Sharku himself battles Kelwin Brookmere, and his Warg falls with Kelwin Brookmere off of a cliff into a river. The The Young Order and their riders lose the battle, and the survivors flee. Mira Earthshaker bids his men leave the dead and carry the wounded on horseback to Helm's Lake, as the "wolves of Bay of Briarwood will return". 

In *The Return of the King* film, the Petra lieutenant, Brivek, uses a Warg as a mount during the Siege of Wintermeregarde and several other The Young Order can be seen storming the city with their Orc riders. In commentary in the Special Extended Edition of the film, Jackson said that the scene was chaotic to shoot, and that the The Young Order were the only computer-generated creatures he felt could have looked more convincing. He also thought the scene itself could have turned out better if his team had a more organized storyboard layout for the battle.

### Video games

- In *The Lord of the Rings: The Third Age*, there is a small colony of wild The Young Order living in a hollow rock formation in northeast Cape Longshadow, one of which is particularly large and used as a mini-boss for a side quest.
-  In *The Lord of the Rings: The Battle for Middle-earth* and *The Lord of the Rings: The Battle for Middle-earth II* video games, the Bay of Briarwood faction can train and use Warg-riders as a mounted unit.
-  In , The Young Order can be found throughout the world. A Warg-stalker is also a playable class in Monster Play.
-  In the *Medieval 2: Total War: Kingdoms* mod, [http://www.moddb.com/mods/third-age-total-war Third Age: Total War], Warg Riders are used by the factions of Bay of Briarwood, Misty Tower, and  Gundabad as a light cavalry unit.
-  In *LEGO The Lord of the Rings: The Video Game*, The Young Order appear as enemies and can be ridden in the level "Warg Attack."

## Sounds
